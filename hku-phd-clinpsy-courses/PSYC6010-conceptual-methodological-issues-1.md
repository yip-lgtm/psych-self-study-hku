# PSYC6010 — Conceptual and Methodological Issues in Psychological Research I

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC6010
- **Title:** Conceptual and methodological issues in psychological research I
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year)
- **Year:** 1
- **HKU Description:** First of a two-course sequence examining conceptual, theoretical, and methodological foundations of psychological research. Philosophy of science, epistemology, research paradigms, and their implications for psychological research design. Required for 4-year PhD students.
- **Prerequisites:** PhD program admission
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Foundation of research philosophy; PhD-only course

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Discuss philosophy of science as applied to psychology
2. Compare epistemological positions (positivism, post-positivism, constructivism, pragmatism)
3. Critically evaluate research paradigms
4. Articulate the relationship between theory and method
5. Design rigorous research informed by philosophical commitments
6. Engage with epistemological debates in your subfield

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Philosophy of Science Foundations (5 hours)
- Logical positivism
- Popper and falsificationism
- Kuhn and paradigms
- Lakatos and research programmes
- Feyerabend and anarchism

### Topic 2: Epistemology (5 hours)
- What is knowledge?
- Justified true belief
- Sources of knowledge
- Skepticism

### Topic 3: Research Paradigms in Psychology (6 hours)
- Positivist/empiricist
- Post-positivist
- Constructivist/interpretivist
- Critical theory
- Pragmatism
- Mixed methods as paradigm

### Topic 4: Ontology (4 hours)
- Realism vs. relativism
- Mind-body problem
- Reductionism
- Emergence

### Topic 5: Theory and Method (5 hours)
- Theory-driven vs. data-driven research
- Hypothetico-deductive method
- Abductive reasoning
- Inductive vs. deductive

### Topic 6: Quantitative Methodology and Philosophy (5 hours)
- Measurement theory
- Operationalization
- Statistical inference and epistemology
- Bayesian vs. frequentist

### Topic 7: Qualitative Methodology and Philosophy (4 hours)
- Phenomenology
- Hermeneutics
- Grounded theory
- IPA
- Narrative research

### Topic 8: Mixed Methods (3 hours)
- Paradigm debates
- Integration strategies
- Joint displays

### Topic 9: Open Science and Replication (3 hours)
- Pre-registration
- Open data
- Replication crisis
- Reform efforts

### Topic 10: Research Ethics (3 hours)
- IRB
- Informed consent
- Data management
- Authorship

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare positivism and constructivism.
2. What is falsificationism? Why is it important?
3. Discuss the replication crisis and its epistemological roots.
4. How does your philosophical stance inform your research design?
5. What are the trade-offs of mixed methods research?
