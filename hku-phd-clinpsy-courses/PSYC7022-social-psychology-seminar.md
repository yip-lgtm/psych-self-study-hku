# PSYC7022 — Postgraduate Seminar in Social Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC7022
- **Title:** Postgraduate seminar in social psychology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology; elective for MSocSc Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** Variable
- **HKU Description:** Advanced graduate seminar in social psychology. Current research and theory in social cognition, attitudes, social influence, group processes, prejudice, and prosocial behavior. Emphasis on critical evaluation of methods and theory, with connections to clinical applications.
- **Prerequisites:** Postgrad standing; foundation in social psychology
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** PhD elective; advanced research focus

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate contemporary social psychology research
2. Discuss current theoretical debates
3. Apply advanced methods in social research
4. Connect social psychology to clinical applications
5. Design rigorous social research
6. Communicate social psychology research effectively

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Social cognition | Social information processing |
| Schema | Knowledge structure |
| Stereotype | Beliefs about group |
| Attitude | Evaluation |
| Cognitive dissonance | Discomfort from inconsistency |
| ELM | Elaboration Likelihood Model |
| Conformity | Adapting to group |
| Obedience | Following authority |
| Social identity | Self as group member |
| Bystander effect | Less help with more people |
| Altruism | Helping without benefit |
| Empathy | Understanding others' feelings |
| WEIRD | Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic |
