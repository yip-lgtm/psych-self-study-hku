# PSYC7002 — Assessment of Personality and Psychopathology (PhD)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC7002
- **Title:** Assessment of personality and psychopathology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year)
- **Year:** 1
- **HKU Description:** Doctoral-level theory and practice of personality and psychopathology assessment. Emphasizes research-informed assessment, integration of multiple data sources, and advanced integrative report writing.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7024
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Doctoral-level expectations; research integration

## Learning Objectives (PhD-level)

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply doctoral-level psychometric principles
2. Administer, score, and interpret major personality tests with sophistication
3. Discuss projective personality tests and controversies
4. Conduct advanced clinical interviews
5. Integrate multiple data sources for doctoral-level diagnosis
6. Write doctoral-level integrated reports
7. Apply assessment in diverse populations with cultural competence
8. Connect assessment to research literature

## Topic Outline (PhD-level)

### Topic 1: Foundations of Assessment (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level psychometric principles
- Reliability and validity advanced
- Cross-cultural validity
- Ethical use of tests at doctoral level

### Topic 2: Clinical Interviewing (10 hours)
- Doctoral-level interview skills
- SCID-5
- Mental status exam
- Cultural formulation interview (DSM-5)
- Research-informed interview

### Topic 3: Objective Personality Tests I — MMPI-2/MMPI-2-RF (10 hours)
- Advanced interpretation
- Research on MMPI
- Restructured form

### Topic 4: Objective Personality Tests II — 16PF, NEO-PI-R (8 hours)
- FFM assessment at doctoral level
- NEO-PI-R
- Research on personality assessment

### Topic 5: Other Self-Report Measures (5 hours)
- BDI-II, BAI
- Specific measures
- Cross-cultural validation research

### Topic 6: Projective Tests (5 hours)
- Rorschach (CS) — controversy
- TAT
- Doctoral-level debate
- Evidence base

### Topic 7: Behavioral Assessment (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level functional analysis
- Behavioral observation
- Self-monitoring
- Technology-enhanced assessment

### Topic 8: Integrative Assessment (8 hours)
- Multi-method integration
- Cross-informant integration
- Doctoral-level differential diagnosis
- Research-informed integration

### Topic 9: Doctoral-Level Report Writing (10 hours)
- Comprehensive integrated reports
- Doctoral-level synthesis
- Feedback to clients
- Communication with referrers

### Topic 10: Cultural and Ethical Issues (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level test bias debate
- Cultural validity research
- HK Chinese context
- Indigenous assessment
