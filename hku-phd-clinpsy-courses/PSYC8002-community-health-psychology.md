# PSYC8002 — Community and Health Psychology (PhD)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC8002
- **Title:** Community and health psychology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year)
- **Year:** 1 or 2 (flexible)
- **HKU Description:** Doctoral-level theory and application of community and health psychology. Population-level approaches, prevention, program evaluation, and research. Doctoral-level integration of research and practice.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7024
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Doctoral-level; research focus

## Learning Objectives (PhD-level)

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply doctoral-level community psychology principles
2. Apply doctoral-level health psychology
3. Design doctoral-level prevention programs
4. Conduct community needs assessments with research literacy
5. Develop doctoral-level health promotion interventions
6. Evaluate community programs rigorously
7. Discuss doctoral-level public mental health
8. Conduct doctoral-level research in community/health psychology

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Community psychology | Social/ecological approach |
| Health psychology | Psychology of health/illness |
| Prevention | Pre-empting disorder |
| Universal prevention | Entire population |
| Selective prevention | At-risk groups |
| Indicated prevention | Symptomatic individuals |
| Logic model | Program design framework |
| Biopsychosocial | Bio + psych + social |
| Health Belief Model | Perceived susceptibility/severity |
| Transtheoretical | Stages of change |
| mHealth | Mobile health |
| Public health | Population health approach |
| Implementation science | Research on implementation |
