# PSYC6025 — Postgraduate Seminar in Scientific Writing for Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC6025
- **Title:** Postgraduate seminar in scientific writing for psychology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year); open to other postgraduates
- **Year:** Variable
- **HKU Description:** Advanced seminar in scientific writing for psychology. Covers journal article structure, grant proposal writing, dissertation writing, peer review, and responding to reviewers. Emphasizes clarity, precision, and argumentation.
- **Prerequisites:** Postgrad standing
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework (writing samples, peer review exercises)
- **Notes:** Writing-intensive; supports dissertation and publication

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Write a clear, well-structured journal article
2. Write a compelling grant proposal
3. Write dissertation chapters effectively
4. Provide constructive peer review
5. Respond effectively to reviewer comments
6. Apply APA 7th and submission standards
7. Develop a writing practice and manage writing blocks

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Principles of Scientific Writing (3 hours)
- Clarity, precision, concision
- Argumentation
- Active vs. passive voice
- Hedging and certainty

### Topic 2: Journal Article Structure (5 hours)
- IMRaD
- Title and abstract
- Introduction logic
- Methods precision
- Results presentation
- Discussion structure

### Topic 3: Writing the Introduction (5 hours)
- Funnel structure
- Literature review
- Identifying gap
- Stating contribution
- Hypotheses

### Topic 4: Writing the Methods (4 hours)
- Participants
- Measures
- Procedure
- Analysis plan

### Topic 5: Writing the Results (5 hours)
- Tables and figures
- Statistical reporting (APA 7th)
- Effect sizes and confidence intervals
- Pre-registration alignment

### Topic 6: Writing the Discussion (5 hours)
- Summary of findings
- Interpretation
- Comparison to literature
- Limitations
- Future directions
- Implications

### Topic 7: Grant Proposal Writing (5 hours)
- Specific Aims
- Significance
- Innovation
- Approach
- Budget
- Review criteria

### Topic 8: Dissertation Writing (5 hours)
- Chapter structure
- Literature review
- Method chapters
- Results chapters
- General discussion

### Topic 9: Peer Review (4 hours)
- How to review
- Reviewer mindset
- Constructive feedback
- Editorial decisions

### Topic 10: Responding to Reviewers (4 hours)
- Reading reviews carefully
- Point-by-point responses
- Tone and professionalism
- When to push back

### Topic 11: Writing Process (3 hours)
- Daily writing habits
- Overcoming blocks
- Feedback incorporation
- Revision strategies

### Topic 12: Conference Presentations (2 hours)
- Abstracts
- Posters
- Oral presentations

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Scientific writing | Formal academic prose |
| IMRaD | Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion |
| Funnel structure | Broad to specific |
| Effect size | Standardized magnitude |
| Confidence interval | Range estimate |
| Specific Aims | Grant proposal goal statement |
| Peer review | Expert evaluation |
| Revision | Improving draft |
| Pre-registration | Public hypothesis registry |
| APA 7th | Current APA style |
| Authorship | Substantive contribution credit |
| Plagiarism | Using others' work without credit |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Critique a published paper's structure.
2. Write a Specific Aims page for a grant.
3. Provide peer review for a sample paper.
4. Respond to reviewer comments.
5. Develop a daily writing plan.
