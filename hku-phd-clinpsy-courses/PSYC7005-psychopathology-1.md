# PSYC7005 — Psychopathology I (PhD)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC7005
- **Title:** Psychopathology I
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year)
- **Year:** 1
- **HKU Description:** Doctoral-level introduction to adult psychopathology using DSM-5-TR and emerging frameworks (RDoC). Etiology, phenomenology, course, treatment. Emphasizes research literacy and theoretical integration.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7024
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Doctoral-level; RDoC and research focus

## Learning Objectives (PhD-level)

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply DSM-5-TR diagnostic criteria with sophistication
2. Discuss RDoC as alternative framework
3. Critically evaluate etiology and risk factor research
4. Discuss doctoral-level course and prognosis
5. Apply evidence-based treatments with research literacy
6. Conduct doctoral-level diagnostic interviews
7. Discuss cultural factors at doctoral level
8. Engage with current debates in classification

## Topic Outline (PhD-level)

### Topic 1: Foundations of Psychopathology (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level history
- DSM-5-TR organization
- RDoC framework
- Network theory
- Multidimensional approach
- Cultural formulation

### Topic 2: Depressive Disorders (6 hours)
- Doctoral-level MDD
- Persistent depressive disorder
- Other specified depressive disorders
- Suicide risk
- Research on depression

### Topic 3: Anxiety Disorders (8 hours)
- GAD, panic, agoraphobia
- Social anxiety, specific phobias
- Research on anxiety

### Topic 4: OCD and Related (4 hours)
- OCD
- BDD, hoarding, trichotillomania
- Research on OCD

### Topic 5: Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders (5 hours)
- PTSD
- Acute stress disorder
- Adjustment disorders
- Complex PTSD
- Research on trauma

### Topic 6: Bipolar and Related (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level bipolar I and II
- Cyclothymia
- Differential with depression
- Research on bipolar

### Topic 7: Sleep-Wake Disorders (3 hours)
- Insomnia
- Hypersomnolence
- Parasomnias
- Behavioral treatment
- Research on sleep

### Topic 8: Somatic Symptom and Related (3 hours)
- Somatic symptom disorder
- Illness anxiety
- Conversion
- Health psychology link
- Research

### Topic 9: Substance-Related (3 hours)
- Substance use disorders
- Intoxication and withdrawal
- Behavioral addictions
- Research

### Topic 10: Etiology and Risk Factors (5 hours)
- Biological (genetic, neurotransmitter, neuroimaging)
- Psychological (cognitive, behavioral, psychodynamic)
- Social (stress, trauma, attachment)
- Doctoral-level diathesis-stress
- Biopsychosocial formulation
- Gene-environment interaction
- Epigenetics

### Topic 11: Cultural Considerations (3 hours)
- Doctoral-level cultural variation
- Idioms of distress
- HK Chinese context
- Indigenous psychologies

### Topic 12: Doctoral-Level Clinical Reasoning (3 hours)
- Case formulation
- Differential diagnosis
- Treatment planning
- Integration with research

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Psychopathology | Study of mental disorders |
| DSM-5-TR | Diagnostic manual |
| RDoC | Research Domain Criteria |
| Network theory | Disorders as networks |
| Diagnosis | Clinical determination |
| Differential diagnosis | Distinguishing conditions |
| Comorbidity | Co-occurring disorders |
| Etiology | Cause or origin |
| Risk factor | Variable increasing likelihood |
| Diathesis-stress | Predisposition + stress |
| GxE | Gene-environment interaction |
| Epigenetics | Experience-driven gene changes |
| Biopsychosocial | Bio + psych + social |
| Cultural formulation | Framework for cultural context |
| Idioms of distress | Culture-specific expressions |
