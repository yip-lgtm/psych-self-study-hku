# PSYC7021 — Postgraduate Seminar in Cognitive Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC7021
- **Title:** Postgraduate seminar in cognitive psychology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology; elective for MSocSc Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** Variable
- **HKU Description:** Advanced graduate seminar in cognitive psychology. Current research and theory in attention, perception, memory, language, thinking, and decision making. Emphasis on critical evaluation of methods and theory, with connections to clinical applications.
- **Prerequisites:** Postgrad standing; foundation in cognitive psychology
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** PhD elective; advanced research focus

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate contemporary cognitive psychology research
2. Discuss current theoretical debates
3. Apply advanced methods in cognitive research
4. Connect cognitive psychology to clinical applications
5. Design rigorous cognitive research
6. Communicate cognitive psychology research effectively

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Course Introduction (3 hours)
- Topic selection
- Reading groups
- Presentation schedule

### Topic 2: Attention (4 hours)
- Attention networks
- Selective vs. divided attention
- Sustained attention and vigilance
- Clinical disorders (ADHD, neglect)

### Topic 3: Perception (4 hours)
- Predictive coding
- Multisensory integration
- Object recognition
- Face processing
- Clinical agnosias

### Topic 4: Memory (5 hours)
- Memory systems
- Working memory
- Episodic and semantic
- Memory disorders
- Reconsolidation

### Topic 5: Language (4 hours)
- Speech perception and production
- Reading
- Bilingualism
- Aphasia
- Bilingual aphasia

### Topic 6: Thinking and Reasoning (4 hours)
- Decision making
- Judgment under uncertainty
- Problem solving
- Heuristics and biases
- Dual-process theory

### Topic 7: Cognitive Control (4 hours)
- Executive function
- Inhibitory control
- Cognitive flexibility
- Prefrontal cortex
- Clinical disorders

### Topic 8: Embodied and Situated Cognition (3 hours)
- 4E cognition
- Action and perception
- Extended mind
- Clinical implications

### Topic 9: Cognitive Aging (3 hours)
- Normal cognitive aging
- Compensatory mechanisms
- Cognitive training
- Pathological aging

### Topic 10: Cognitive Neuroscience Methods (4 hours)
- fMRI, EEG, MEG
- Single-unit recording (animal)
- TMS, tDCS
- Mobile neuroimaging
- Computational modeling

### Topic 11: Consciousness (3 hours)
- Neural correlates
- Theories
- Disorders of consciousness
- Clinical implications

### Topic 12: Research Directions (3 hours)
- Open questions
- Methodological innovations
- Clinical translation
