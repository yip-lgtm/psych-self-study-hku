# PSYC8004 — Clinical Neuropsychology (PhD)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC8004
- **Title:** Clinical neuropsychology
- **Program:** PhD Clinical Psychology (4-year)
- **Year:** 2
- **HKU Description:** Doctoral-level clinical applications of neuropsychology. Advanced neuroanatomy, neuropsychological assessment, differential diagnosis, rehabilitation, and research integration.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7018, PSYC7006
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Doctoral-level clinical neuropsychology

## Learning Objectives (PhD-level)

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply doctoral-level neuroanatomy to clinical assessment
2. Conduct comprehensive neuropsychological evaluation with research literacy
3. Differentially diagnose neurological conditions at doctoral level
4. Apply doctoral-level neuropsychological rehabilitation
5. Interpret neuroimaging findings with sophistication
6. Communicate with medical professionals at doctoral level
7. Discuss cultural and ethical considerations
8. Conduct neuropsychology research

## Topic Outline (PhD-level)

### Topic 1: Neuroanatomy Review (5 hours)
- Doctoral-level cortical and subcortical
- White matter tracts
- Vascular territories
- Functional networks
- Neuroanatomy research

### Topic 2: Neurological Conditions (8 hours)
- Doctoral-level stroke
- TBI
- Brain tumors
- Epilepsy
- MS, movement disorders
- Dementia
- Research

### Topic 3: Comprehensive Neuropsychological Battery (8 hours)
- Doctoral-level test selection
- Fixed vs. flexible battery
- Premorbid estimation
- Performance validity
- Research on batteries

### Topic 4: Domain-Specific Assessment (10 hours)
- Doctoral-level attention/processing
- Memory
- Language
- Visuospatial
- Executive function
- Mood and personality

### Topic 5: Doctoral-Level Differential Diagnosis (6 hours)
- Dementia vs. pseudodementia
- MCI
- Alzheimer's vs. vascular
- FTD variants
- Lewy body
- Research

### Topic 6: Neuroimaging Integration (5 hours)
- Doctoral-level CT/MRI
- fMRI, FDG-PET
- White matter changes
- Reporting
- Imaging research

### Topic 7: Rehabilitation (5 hours)
- Doctoral-level cognitive rehabilitation
- Compensatory strategies
- Environmental modification
- Family education
- Return to work/school
- Research

### Topic 8: Pediatric Neuropsychology (3 hours)
- Doctoral-level developmental
- Pediatric TBI
- Neurodevelopmental conditions
- School liaison

### Topic 9: Geriatric Neuropsychology (3 hours)
- Doctoral-level normal aging
- Mild NCD
- Dementia
- Capacity assessment

### Topic 10: Forensic and Capacity (3 hours)
- Doctoral-level testamentary capacity
- Fitness to plead
- Compensation evaluations
- Independent medical examinations

### Topic 11: Communication and Report Writing (4 hours)
- Doctoral-level medical team communication
- Comprehensive reports
- Feedback to patients/families
- Research integration

### Topic 12: Cultural and Ethical Issues (3 hours)
- Doctoral-level test bias research
- HK Chinese considerations
- Consent and capacity
- End-of-life considerations
