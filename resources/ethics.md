# Resources — Research Ethics

## CITI Program (free for individuals)

- **URL:** https://about.citiprogram.org
- **Register as:** "Independent Learner" (free)
- **Required modules** (Month 5):
  - **Responsible Conduct of Research (RCR)** — social/behavioral track
  - **Human Subjects Research** (if doing your own data collection)
  - **Information Privacy & Security** (if handling identifiable data)

## HKU-Specific (if collaborating with HKU faculty)

- HKU requires ethics review (IREC) for any research involving human subjects.
- For your capstone (self-run, no HKU affiliation): CITI is sufficient as evidence of ethics training.
- **However:** if you publish or apply with the data, disclose the ethics training and informed consent process.

## For Your Capstone (Month 6)

### Required Ethics Documentation
- [ ] Informed consent form (online, first page of survey)
- [ ] Information sheet (what the study is, who you are, contact, withdrawal rights)
- [ ] Debriefing page (after submission)
- [ ] Data handling plan (anonymization, retention period, storage)
- [ ] CITI completion certificate (PDF copy in `ethics/`)

### Ethical Principles to Apply
1. **Voluntary participation** — no coercion
2. **Informed consent** — participants know what they're signing up for
3. **Right to withdraw** — at any time, no penalty
4. **Anonymity & confidentiality** — no identifying info in datasets
5. **Data protection** — encrypted storage, no public sharing of raw data without consent
6. **No harm** — avoid distressing content; have support resources listed if mental health topics

### Special Considerations for Your Background
- Engineer + self-learner = risk of being seen as "amateur"
- **Mitigation:** show you understand the ethical framework, not just the method
- Include CITI certificate in application package
