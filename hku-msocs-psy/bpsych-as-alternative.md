# BPsych as Alternative Path (4-Year Fallback)

> **Read this only if MSocSc(Psy) conversion doesn't work out.** Otherwise, stay focused on the 1-year MSocSc path.

---

## Why This File Exists

The 6-month self-study plan targets HKU MSocSc(Psy) conversion (1 year). But sometimes:
- Application is rejected
- You realize you want a deeper foundation
- You have the financial runway to invest 4 years
- You want an undergraduate peer cohort

If any of these apply, **BPsych is your backup plan**. This file shows you what that path looks like.

---

## BPsych Quick Facts

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4 years full-time (8 semesters) |
| **Total credits** | 240 minimum |
| **Major credits** | 84 (Psychology) |
| **Tuition (2026-27 est.)** | ~HK$209,800 total (similar to MSocSc) |
| **Honours target for PhD** | First Class (3.60+ GGPA) |
| **What you get** | UG degree + 1-year postgrad research training in one go |

> **Reality check:** Most conversion applicants have already completed an UG degree. Doing another full UG is a 4-year detour. Only do this if the 1-year route is genuinely blocked.

---

## When BPsych Makes Sense

✅ You genuinely want a fresh start in psychology with full undergraduate experience
✅ You have the time + money (4 years, ~HK$210k, no significant income)
✅ You want a strong cohort + lab access + supervised research from year 1
✅ You're considering an academic career and want UG research experience on transcript

❌ Don't do BPsych just because you're scared of MSocSc(Psy) rejection
❌ Don't do BPsych if you have a clear subfield already (you can target PhD via MPhil/MSocSc)
❌ Don't do BPsych if you need to stay in the workforce

---

## How Your 6-Month Self-Study Maps to BPsych Year 1

If you do BPsych, your self-study would have given you:
- **Year 1 head start:** you'd skip intro courses (PSYC1001, 1004, 2000-level survey)
- **Free electives:** you could take advanced electives in Year 1
- **Capstone advantage:** you can start your capstone project (12 credits) in Year 3 instead of Year 4

Roughly 30–50% of BPsych content would feel "review" to you after the 6-month self-study. The 50% that's new: lab work, cohort discussion, supervised research, advanced stats (SEM, multilevel modeling), clinical exposure (if clinical track).

---

## Modified Self-Study if Targeting BPsych

If you decide BPsych is the right path:
- **Spend more time on lab skills** (find online psychophysics experiments, try to replicate)
- **Build relationships with potential referees EARLY** (HKU faculty you'll want to write for you in Year 4)
- **Apply for research assistant positions** at HKU (paid, part-time) during BPsych
- **Target a First Class GPA** — be in the top 10% of the cohort

## Decision Framework

```
Are you 100% sure you want a research/academic career in psychology?
├── NO → Don't do BPsych. Do MSocSc(Psy) or just stay in engineering.
│
└── YES → Are you willing to invest 4 years + HK$210k?
    ├── NO → Do MSocSc(Psy) (1 year) → MPhil/PhD
    │
    └── YES → Do you have a strong application for MSocSc(Psy)?
        ├── NO → Do BPsych (4 years), then PhD
        └── YES → Do MSocSc(Psy) (1 year), then PhD
```

**Default answer for an engineering grad with self-study discipline:** MSocSc(Psy) → PhD.

---

## Hybrid Path (If You Change Mind Mid-Stream)

If you start MSocSc(Psy) and decide to do BPsych instead:
- You can apply to transfer into BPsych Year 2 (with credit transfer, max 50% = 120 credits)
- Your MSocSc(Psy) courses might count toward BPsych elective requirements
- This is messy but possible — talk to HKU Academic Services early

## What This Means for Your 6-Month Self-Study

**The self-study plan is BPsych-friendly.** Even if you pivot:
- All Month 1–4 content is BPsych core
- Capstone experience maps to BPsych capstone (12 credits)
- Stats + methods competency is BPsych-required
- The 6-month deliverable still makes you a stronger candidate for BOTH paths

So: **keep executing the 6-month plan regardless of which degree you target.**
