# PSYC7303 — Biological Psychology

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC7303
- **Title:** Biological psychology
- **MSocSc(Psy) Compulsory:** Yes (one of 8)
- **BPsych Equiv:** PSYC2022
- **Self-Study Month:** 2
- **Estimated Study Hours:** 40–50 hours

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe the structure and function of the nervous system
2. Explain how neurons communicate (action potentials, synaptic transmission)
3. Identify major neurotransmitter systems and their behavioral effects
4. Describe the major brain regions and their functions
5. Explain the endocrine system's role in behavior
6. Discuss the methods used to study brain-behavior relationships
7. Apply biological psychology to behavior (e.g., aggression, emotion, cognition)
8. Discuss nature-nurture interactions in behavior
9. Critically evaluate claims about brain-behavior relationships

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: History and Approaches (2 hours)
- Phrenology to modern neuroscience
- Reductionism vs. holism
- Biopsychosocial model
- Mind-body problem (Descartes, dualism, monism)

### Topic 2: Neuroanatomy — Overview (5 hours)
- Central vs. peripheral nervous system
- Forebrain, midbrain, hindbrain
- Cerebral cortex (4 lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital)
- Subcortical structures (thalamus, hypothalamus, basal ganglia, amygdala, hippocampus)
- Brainstem and cerebellum
- Spinal cord

### Topic 3: The Neuron (5 hours)
- Structure (dendrites, soma, axon, terminal buttons)
- Glial cells (astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia)
- Action potential (resting potential, depolarization, repolarization, hyperpolarization)
- All-or-none principle
- Refractory periods
- Saltatory conduction

### Topic 4: Synaptic Transmission (5 hours)
- Electrical vs. chemical synapses
- Neurotransmitter release (vesicles, exocytosis)
- Receptor types (ionotropic, metabotropic)
- Excitatory vs. inhibitory postsynaptic potentials
- Reuptake, enzymatic degradation
- Neuromodulators

### Topic 5: Neurotransmitter Systems (5 hours)
- Acetylcholine (muscle, memory)
- Dopamine (reward, movement, schizophrenia)
- Serotonin (mood, sleep, appetite)
- Norepinephrine (arousal, stress)
- GABA (inhibition, anxiety)
- Glutamate (excitation, learning)
- Endorphins (pain, pleasure)

### Topic 6: The Endocrine System (4 hours)
- Pituitary gland (master gland)
- Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis
- Cortisol and stress
- Thyroid, reproductive hormones
- Hormone-behavior interactions

### Topic 7: Brain-Behavior Relationships I — Emotion (4 hours)
- Amygdala and fear
- Limbic system
- Prefrontal cortex and emotion regulation
- James-Lange vs. Cannon-Bard vs. Schachter-Singer

### Topic 8: Brain-Behavior Relationships II — Memory (3 hours)
- Hippocampus and declarative memory
- Cerebellum and procedural memory
- Amygdala and emotional memory
- Synaptic plasticity (LTP, LTD)
- Hebb's rule

### Topic 9: Brain-Behavior Relationships III — Language & Motor (3 hours)
- Broca's area (speech production)
- Wernicke's area (speech comprehension)
- Lateralization
- Motor cortex, premotor cortex
- Basal ganglia and movement disorders (Parkinson's, Huntington's)

### Topic 10: Behavioral Genetics (4 hours)
- Nature vs. nurture (false dichotomy)
- Heritability (h²) — what it means and doesn't
- Twin studies (MZ vs. DZ)
- Adoption studies
- GWAS (genome-wide association studies)
- Gene-environment interactions (GxE)

### Topic 11: Methods in Biological Psychology (4 hours)
- EEG (electroencephalography) — temporal resolution
- fMRI (functional MRI) — spatial resolution
- PET scans
- TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation)
- Lesion studies
- Animal models
- Optogenetics

### Topic 12: Plasticity and Neurogenesis (3 hours)
- Neuroplasticity across lifespan
- Critical periods
- Adult neurogenesis (hippocampus, olfactory bulb)
- Experience-dependent plasticity
- Brain damage and recovery

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Neuron | Specialized cell that transmits electrical and chemical signals |
| Action potential | Brief electrical signal traveling down an axon (all-or-none) |
| Synapse | Junction between two neurons (or neuron and effector cell) |
| Neurotransmitter | Chemical messenger released at synapse |
| Reuptake | Reabsorption of neurotransmitter by the presynaptic neuron |
| Myelin sheath | Insulating layer around axons (speeds conduction) |
| Limbic system | Network involved in emotion and memory |
| Amygdala | Almond-shaped nucleus; fear and emotional processing |
| Hippocampus | Seahorse-shaped structure; declarative memory formation |
| Prefrontal cortex | Anterior frontal lobe; executive function, decision-making |
| HPA axis | Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal system; stress response |
| Cortisol | Stress hormone released by adrenal cortex |
| Heritability | Proportion of variance in a trait due to genetic differences (0–1) |
| Neuroplasticity | Brain's ability to change structure/function with experience |
| Lateralization | Specialization of function in one hemisphere vs. the other |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Trace the path of a sensory signal from the skin to the cerebral cortex.
2. Explain how an action potential is generated. What ions move in which directions?
3. Differentiate between ionotropic and metabotropic receptors. Give an example of each.
4. Describe the HPA axis response to a stressor. What are the long-term effects of chronic cortisol elevation?
5. What does it mean to say heritability of IQ is ~0.5? What are 3 common misunderstandings?
6. How do fMRI and EEG differ in what they measure? What are the trade-offs?
7. Patient H.M. had his hippocampus removed. What could he still learn? What couldn't he?
8. Describe the role of dopamine in reward learning. How does this relate to addiction?
9. Explain Hebb's rule. How does it relate to LTP?
10. What is the difference between "the brain" and "the mind"? Why is this question still debated?

## Sample Discussion Questions

- Phineas Gage's frontal lobe damage changed his personality. What does this tell us about the biological basis of personality?
- SSRIs increase serotonin and take 4–6 weeks to relieve depression. Why the delay? What does this reveal about neurotransmitter function?
- Discuss the ethical implications of using TMS to enhance cognitive performance in healthy individuals.
- Twin studies show high heritability for many psychological traits. Why is this NOT evidence that environment doesn't matter?
- Compare and contrast: lesion studies, fMRI, and TMS as methods for understanding brain-behavior relationships.
