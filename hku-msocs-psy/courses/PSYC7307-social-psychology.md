# PSYC7307 — Social Psychology

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC7307
- **Title:** Social psychology
- **MSocSc(Psy) Compulsory:** Yes (one of 8)
- **BPsych Equiv:** PSYC2020
- **Self-Study Month:** 3
- **Estimated Study Hours:** 35–45 hours

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Explain how people perceive, think about, and relate to others
2. Describe major social influence processes (conformity, obedience, persuasion)
3. Discuss group dynamics and decision making
4. Apply social psychology to real-world issues (prejudice, aggression, prosocial behavior)
5. Evaluate research methods in social psychology
6. Connect social psychology to other subfields
7. Critically evaluate classic studies for ethical and methodological issues
8. Discuss cultural influences on social behavior

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Social Psychology (2 hours)
- Definition and scope
- Person vs. situation (strong situation argument)
- History (Triplett 1898; Sherif, Asch, Lewin)
- Research methods (experiments, field studies, surveys)

### Topic 2: Social Cognition I — Person Perception (4 hours)
- Attribution theory (internal vs. external; Kelley, Jones & Davis)
- Fundamental attribution error
- Correspondence bias
- Actor-observer asymmetry
- Self-serving bias
- Impression formation (central vs. peripheral traits)

### Topic 3: Social Cognition II — Attitudes and Persuasion (4 hours)
- Tripartite model (cognitive, affective, behavioral)
- Attitude measurement (self-report, implicit)
- Cognitive dissonance (Festinger)
- Self-perception theory (Bem)
- Elaboration Likelihood Model (Petty & Cacioppo)
- Heuristic-Systematic Model (Chaiken)
- Yale approach (Hovland)

### Topic 4: Conformity and Compliance (4 hours)
- Sherif (1936) — autokinetic effect, group norms
- Asch (1951) — line judgment, conformity pressure
- Factors affecting conformity (group size, unanimity, status, culture)
- Compliance techniques (foot-in-the-door, door-in-the-face, lowball, scarcity)
- Compliance practitioners (Cialdini's principles)

### Topic 5: Obedience to Authority (3 hours)
- Milgram (1963) — shock study
- Factors increasing obedience (proximity, legitimacy, uniforms)
- Modern replications and ethical critiques
- Stanford Prison Experiment (Zimbardo) — methodological and ethical issues
- Resistance to authority

### Topic 6: Group Dynamics (4 hours)
- Group polarization
- Groupthink (Janis)
- Social facilitation and inhibition
- Social loafing
- Deindividuation
- Bystander effect (Darley & Latané)
- Group decision-making (hidden profile problem)

### Topic 7: Aggression (3 hours)
- Definitions (hostile vs. instrumental)
- Biological factors (testosterone, serotonin)
- Social learning theory (Bandura's Bobo doll)
- Frustration-aggression hypothesis
- General Aggression Model
- Media violence effects (research evidence)
- Situational triggers

### Topic 8: Prosocial Behavior (3 hours)
- Altruism vs. egoism
- Bystander intervention
- Empathy-altruism hypothesis (Batson)
- Social exchange theory
- Norms (social responsibility, reciprocity)
- Cultural differences

### Topic 9: Prejudice and Stereotyping (4 hours)
- Definitions and distinctions
- Origins (social learning, social identity)
- Stereotype threat (Steele & Aronson)
- Implicit bias (IAT)
- Modern vs. old-fashioned racism
- Reducing prejudice (contact hypothesis, jigsaw classroom)

### Topic 10: Attraction and Close Relationships (4 hours)
- Factors: proximity, similarity, physical attractiveness
- Attractiveness stereotypes (halo effect)
- Attachment styles in adult relationships
- Social exchange theory
- Sternberg's triangular theory (intimacy, passion, commitment)
- Relationship maintenance

### Topic 11: Social Identity and Intergroup Relations (3 hours)
- Social Identity Theory (Tajfel & Turner)
- In-groups and out-groups
- Minimal group paradigm
- Discrimination
- Strategies for improving intergroup relations

### Topic 12: Cultural Social Psychology (2 hours)
- Individualism vs. collectivism (Hofstede, Triandis)
- East-West differences in attribution, self-concept
- WEIRD samples (Henrich et al., 2010) — limitation
- Cultural neuroscience
- Global applications

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Fundamental attribution error | Tendency to overemphasize dispositional factors for others' behavior |
| Cognitive dissonance | Discomfort from holding two inconsistent cognitions; motivates attitude change |
| Conformity | Adjusting behavior or attitudes to align with group norms |
| Obedience | Compliance with direct orders from authority |
| Compliance | Agreeing to a request from another person |
| Groupthink | Desire for harmony leads to poor decisions |
| Bystander effect | Presence of others reduces helping behavior |
| Social facilitation | Improved performance on simple/well-learned tasks in presence of others |
| Social loafing | Reduced effort in group tasks |
| Deindividuation | Loss of self-awareness and individual identity in groups |
| Stereotype threat | Anxiety about confirming a negative stereotype about one's group |
| In-group bias | Favoring members of one's own group |
| Halo effect | Overall impression of a person influences specific judgments |
| Self-fulfilling prophecy | Beliefs about others lead to behaviors that confirm those beliefs |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Explain the fundamental attribution error. Construct an example.
2. Describe cognitive dissonance. Give 3 real-world examples.
3. Outline the Asch conformity study. What factors increased conformity?
4. What did Milgram's obedience study show? What are the ethical concerns?
5. Compare and contrast: conformity, compliance, and obedience.
6. What is groupthink? Give a historical example (Janis used several).
7. Explain the bystander effect. What is the decision tree (Darley & Latané)?
8. Discuss Bandura's Bobo doll experiments. What are the implications for media violence?
9. Define stereotype threat. How would you design a study to test it?
10. How do cultural differences (individualism vs. collectivism) shape attribution patterns?

## Sample Discussion Questions

- Zimbardo's Stanford Prison Experiment was halted after 6 days due to extreme harm. Discuss the ethical and methodological issues.
- Cognitive dissonance theory has been applied to consumer behavior, post-decision regret, and cult recruitment. Discuss.
- The replication crisis has hit social psychology hard (many classic effects have failed to replicate). Why might this field be especially vulnerable?
- Stereotype threat research is controversial (some say it doesn't replicate). What are the implications for educational policy?
- Hofstede's cultural dimensions are widely used in business. What are the limitations of this approach?
