# PSYC7319 — Introduction to Psychopathology

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC7319
- **Title:** Introduction to psychopathology
- **MSocSc(Psy) Compulsory:** Yes (one of 8)
- **BPsych Equiv:** PSYC2062 (Abnormal Psychology)
- **Self-Study Month:** 4
- **Estimated Study Hours:** 40–50 hours

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe the major frameworks for classifying mental disorders (DSM-5-TR, ICD-11, RDoC)
2. Identify the symptoms and prevalence of major categories of mental disorder
3. Discuss the etiology of psychopathology using a biopsychosocial framework
4. Apply the diathesis-stress model to case conceptualization
5. Critically evaluate the strengths and limitations of categorical diagnosis
6. Demonstrate awareness of cultural factors in psychopathology
7. Discuss ethical issues in diagnosis and treatment
8. Write a case conceptualization using a structured format

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Historical Perspectives and Classification (4 hours)
- History of "madness" — supernatural → moral → medical models
- The development of DSM (I → 5-TR)
- ICD-11 (WHO)
- Categorical vs. dimensional approaches
- RDoC (Research Domain Criteria) — NIMH framework
- Reliability vs. validity in diagnosis

### Topic 2: The DSM-5-TR Framework (3 hours)
- Structure: disorders, criteria, severity specifiers
- Multiaxial system (abandoned in DSM-5)
- Levels of certainty (provisional, definite)
- "V codes" / "Other specified" / "Unspecified"
- Critiques: medicalization, cultural bias, comorbidity

### Topic 3: Etiology — The Biopsychosocial Model (4 hours)
- Biological factors (genetics, neurochemistry, brain structure)
- Psychological factors (cognition, learning, trauma, attachment)
- Social factors (stress, support, culture, SES)
- Diathesis-stress model
- Gene-environment interactions

### Topic 4: Anxiety Disorders (5 hours)
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
- Panic Disorder
- Specific Phobias
- Social Anxiety Disorder
- Agoraphobia
- Etiology: fear conditioning, cognitive biases, neurobiology
- Treatment: CBT, SSRIs, exposure

### Topic 5: Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders (3 hours)
- OCD (obsessions + compulsions)
- Body Dysmorphic Disorder
- Hoarding Disorder
- Trichotillomania
- Etiology: cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit
- Treatment: ERP (exposure + response prevention), SSRIs

### Topic 6: Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders (3 hours)
- PTSD
- Acute Stress Disorder
- Adjustment Disorders
- Etiology: trauma exposure, peri-traumatic processing, post-traumatic cognitions
- Treatment: trauma-focused CBT, EMDR, SSRIs

### Topic 7: Depressive Disorders (4 hours)
- Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
- Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
- Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder
- Seasonal Pattern
- Etiology: monoamine hypothesis, HPA axis, cognitive triad
- Treatment: CBT, IPT, SSRIs/SNRIs, behavioral activation

### Topic 8: Bipolar and Related Disorders (3 hours)
- Bipolar I (mania + depression)
- Bipolar II (hypomania + depression)
- Cyclothymia
- Etiology: circadian rhythm, genetics
- Treatment: mood stabilizers, lithium

### Topic 9: Schizophrenia Spectrum Disorders (5 hours)
- Symptoms: positive (hallucinations, delusions), negative (flat affect, avolition), disorganized
- Schizoaffective, schizophreniform, delusional disorder
- Etiology: dopamine hypothesis, glutamate, neurodevelopmental, environmental (urban birth, cannabis)
- Treatment: antipsychotics (typical, atypical), psychosocial interventions
- Early intervention and prodrome

### Topic 10: Personality Disorders (4 hours)
- DSM-5-TR clusters: A (odd/eccentric), B (dramatic/erratic), C (anxious/fearful)
- Borderline, Antisocial, Narcissistic, Avoidant, Dependent, OCPD
- Alternative: ICD-11 dimensional model
- Etiology: temperament + environment
- Treatment: DBT (borderline), schema therapy

### Topic 11: Substance Use and Eating Disorders (3 hours)
- Substance Use Disorder (criteria)
- Alcohol Use Disorder
- Anorexia Nervosa, Bulimia Nervosa, Binge Eating Disorder
- Etiology: reinforcement, dopaminergic pathways, body image
- Treatment: motivational interviewing, CBT-E, 12-step

### Topic 12: Case Conceptualization (3 hours)
- The 5 P's: Presenting, Predisposing, Precipitating, Perpetuating, Protective
- Diathesis-stress formulation
- Writing a structured case conceptualization
- Cultural formulation (DSM-5-TR Outline for Cultural Formulation)
- Ethical considerations

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Psychopathology | Pattern of behavior or mental process associated with distress or impairment |
| Prevalence | Proportion of population with the disorder at a given time |
| Incidence | Rate of new cases over a period |
| Comorbidity | Co-occurrence of two or more disorders |
| Etiology | Cause or set of causes of a disorder |
| Biopsychosocial | Framework integrating biological, psychological, and social factors |
| Diathesis-stress | Model where predisposition (diathesis) interacts with environmental stress |
| Comorbidity | Co-occurrence of disorders |
| Neurotic vs. psychotic | Outdated: neurotic = anxiety/depression; psychotic = loss of reality testing |
| Positive symptoms | Additions to normal experience (hallucinations, delusions) |
| Negative symptoms | Subtractions from normal experience (flat affect, avolition) |
| Psychosis | Loss of contact with reality |
| Neurosis | Outdated term for anxiety-based disorders (no longer in DSM) |
| Stigma | Negative attitudes toward people with mental illness |
| Recovery model | Person-centered, empowerment-based approach to mental health |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare and contrast the DSM-5-TR categorical approach and the RDoC dimensional approach. What are the trade-offs?
2. Explain the biopsychosocial model. Apply it to one disorder of your choice.
3. Describe the diathesis-stress model. How would you apply it to depression?
4. Differentiate between GAD, Panic Disorder, and Social Anxiety Disorder.
5. What are the 5 P's of case conceptualization? Apply them to a hypothetical case.
6. Discuss the dopamine hypothesis of schizophrenia. What evidence supports it? What are its limitations?
7. Compare the categorical and dimensional models of personality disorders. Why is the field moving toward dimensional?
8. What is comorbidity? Why is it so common in psychopathology? Give 2 examples.
9. Discuss cultural factors in the expression and diagnosis of mental disorders. Why is this important?
10. The medicalization of normal distress (e.g., "shyness" → social anxiety disorder) is controversial. Discuss the ethical implications.

## Connection to Capstone

Psychopathology is **less central** to most applied capstones, BUT it matters if:

- Your topic involves **distress, stress, or mental health** (e.g., trader burnout, depression in engineers)
- You need to **screen for psychiatric history** in your participants (ethics)
- You want to use **psychometric tools** (which often overlap with clinical instruments)
- Your subfield is **clinical psychology** (downstream goal after MSocSc)

**Strong fit topics:**
- "Stress, personality, and risk preferences in solo trading" (anxiety + depression screening important)
- Any capstone involving vulnerable populations (need ethics review)

**Action item:** If your capstone touches on sensitive topics, use PSS (Perceived Stress Scale) rather than clinical measures to avoid triggering distress.

## Sample Discussion Questions

- The DSM-5-TR has been criticized for "medicalizing" normal human variation. Discuss.
- Some disorders (e.g., depression) show cross-cultural consistency; others (e.g., anorexia) are culture-bound. What does this tell us?
- Should personality disorders be treated as medical conditions? What are the arguments for and against?
- The "chemical imbalance" theory of depression has been widely promoted but is now contested. Discuss the implications.
- "Recovery" from serious mental illness (e.g., schizophrenia) is now seen as possible for many. What factors contribute?

## Capstone Deliverable (Month 4 Milestone)

By end of Month 4, write a 1-page case conceptualization:

- Pick a fictional case (real public figure, movie character, or hypothetical)
- Use the 5 P's framework
- Apply the diathesis-stress model
- Write in non-clinical language (no DSM diagnosis required)
- Save to `capstone/case-conceptualization.md`

This is good practice for clinical thinking even if you don't go clinical.
