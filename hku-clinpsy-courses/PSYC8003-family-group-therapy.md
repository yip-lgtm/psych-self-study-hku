# PSYC8003 — Family and Group Therapy

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC8003
- **Title:** Family and group therapy
- **Program:** MSocSc in Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** 2
- **HKU Description:** Theory and practice of family therapy and group psychotherapy. Major family therapy models (Bowen, structural, strategic, systemic, narrative), group therapy models, and applications to clinical populations.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7004
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Builds on individual psychotherapy skills

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major family therapy models
2. Apply systemic formulation
3. Conduct family therapy interventions
4. Describe major group therapy models
5. Lead group therapy interventions
6. Discuss evidence base
7. Adapt therapy to family/group context

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Foundations of Family Therapy (4 hours)
- History of family therapy
- Systems theory basics
- Family life cycle
- HK Chinese family context

### Topic 2: Bowen Family Systems (5 hours)
- Differentiation of self
- Triangles
- Multigenerational transmission
- Family diagrams

### Topic 3: Structural Family Therapy (5 hours)
- Minuchin's structural model
- Boundaries and subsystems
- Enactment
- Joining and restructuring

### Topic 4: Strategic Family Therapy (4 hours)
- Problem-focused approach
- Directives
- Paradoxical interventions

### Topic 5: Other Family Therapy Models (6 hours)
- Solution-focused brief therapy
- Narrative therapy
- Emotionally focused therapy (EFT)
- Milan systemic
- Attachment-based

### Topic 6: Systemic Formulation (5 hours)
- Genograms
- Circular questioning
- Hypothesizing
- Neutrality

### Topic 7: Group Therapy Foundations (5 hours)
- Curative factors (Yalom)
- Group development stages
- Group composition
- Pre-group preparation

### Topic 8: Group Therapy Models (5 hours)
- CBT groups
- Interpersonal groups
- Psychodynamic groups
- DBT skills groups
- Support groups

### Topic 9: Group Leadership Skills (5 hours)
- Managing group process
- Conflict resolution
- Member selection
- Termination

### Topic 10: Cultural and Ethical Issues (3 hours)
- Family therapy in HK Chinese context
- Filial piety
- Multi-generational dynamics
- Confidentiality in groups

### Topic 11: Evidence Base (3 hours)

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Systems theory | Family as interconnected system |
| Differentiation | Balance of self and togetherness |
| Triangles | Three-person emotional system |
| Boundary | Rules defining family subsystems |
| Enactment | Live family interaction in session |
| Genogram | Multi-generational family diagram |
| Circular questioning | Non-blaming inquiry |
| Solution-focused | Building solutions, not solving problems |
| Narrative | Stories that shape identity |
| EFT | Emotionally focused therapy |
| Yalom factors | Curative factors in group therapy |
| Group cohesion | Bond among group members |
