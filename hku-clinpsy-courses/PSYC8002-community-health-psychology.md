# PSYC8002 — Community and Health Psychology

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC8002
- **Title:** Community and health psychology
- **Program:** MSocSc in Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** 1 or 2 (flexible)
- **HKU Description:** Theory and application of community psychology and health psychology in clinical practice. Prevention, promotion, community interventions, public mental health, and population-level approaches.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7024
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Population and prevention focus

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe community psychology principles
2. Apply health psychology to clinical practice
3. Design prevention programs
4. Conduct community needs assessments
5. Develop health promotion interventions
6. Evaluate community programs
7. Discuss public mental health

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Community Psychology Foundations (4 hours)
- History and principles
- Ecological levels of analysis
- Empowerment
- Social capital
- HK context

### Topic 2: Health Psychology Foundations (4 hours)
- Biopsychosocial model
- Stress and illness
- Health behaviors
- Adherence
- Mind-body connection

### Topic 3: Prevention Models (5 hours)
- Universal, selective, indicated prevention
- Public health approach
- Examples (e.g., suicide prevention, depression prevention)

### Topic 4: Health Promotion (4 hours)
- Theories (Health Belief Model, Transtheoretical Model)
- Behavior change techniques
- Communication
- Digital health

### Topic 5: Community Needs Assessment (4 hours)
- Methods (surveys, focus groups, key informant interviews)
- Asset mapping
- Data analysis

### Topic 6: Program Development (5 hours)
- Logic models
- Intervention design
- Implementation
- Cultural adaptation

### Topic 7: Program Evaluation (5 hours)
- Process evaluation
- Outcome evaluation
- Cost-effectiveness
- Reporting

### Topic 8: Health Behavior Change (4 hours)
- Smoking cessation
- Physical activity
- Diet
- Sleep
- Substance use

### Topic 9: Chronic Illness and Psychology (4 hours)
- Diabetes
- Cardiovascular disease
- Cancer
- Pain management
- Palliative care

### Topic 10: Public Mental Health (3 hours)
- Epidemiology of mental disorders
- Mental health policy
- Stigma reduction
- Mental health literacy

### Topic 11: Community Interventions (4 hours)
- School-based
- Workplace
- Primary care integration
- Digital interventions

### Topic 12: Cultural and Ethical Considerations (3 hours)
- HK Chinese context
- Vulnerable populations
- Resource allocation
- Research ethics

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Community psychology | Social/ecological approach |
| Health psychology | Psychology of health and illness |
| Prevention | Pre-empting disorder |
| Universal prevention | Entire population |
| Selective prevention | At-risk groups |
| Indicated prevention | Symptomatic individuals |
| Logic model | Program design framework |
| Biopsychosocial | Bio + psych + social |
| Health Belief Model | Perceived susceptibility/severity |
| Transtheoretical | Stages of change model |
| Health promotion | Enhancing health |
| Public health | Population health approach |
