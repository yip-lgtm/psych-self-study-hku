# PSYC7002 — Assessment of Personality and Psychopathology

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC7002
- **Title:** Assessment of personality and psychopathology
- **Program:** MSocSc in Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** 1
- **HKU Description:** Theory and practice of personality and psychopathology assessment in clinical settings. Covers objective and projective personality tests, structured and unstructured interviews, behavioral observation, and integrative report writing.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7024
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework (typically test administration/scoring, interpretation reports, integrated reports)
- **Notes:** Combines test-based and interview-based assessment

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Administer, score, and interpret major objective personality tests
2. Discuss projective personality tests and their use
3. Conduct structured and unstructured clinical interviews
4. Integrate multiple sources of data for diagnosis
5. Write comprehensive integrated reports
6. Apply assessment in diverse populations

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Foundations of Assessment (4 hours)
- Psychometric principles
- Reliability and validity
- Cultural considerations
- Ethical use of tests

### Topic 2: Clinical Interviewing (10 hours)
- Unstructured interview
- Semi-structured interviews (SCID, MINI)
- Mental status exam
- Cultural formulation interview (DSM-5)

### Topic 3: Objective Personality Tests I — MMPI-2/MMPI-2-RF (10 hours)
- History and development
- Administration and scoring
- Interpretation (clinical, validity, content scales)
- MMPI-2-RF (restructured form)

### Topic 4: Objective Personality Tests II — 16PF, NEO-PI-R (8 hours)
- Five-factor model assessment
- NEO-PI-R administration and interpretation
- 16PF alternative
- Comparative use

### Topic 5: Other Self-Report Measures (5 hours)
- BDI-II, BAI
- Symptom Checklist-90
- Measures for specific conditions
- Cross-cultural validation

### Topic 6: Projective Tests (5 hours)
- Rorschach (CS)
- TAT
- Draw-a-Person
- Use and controversy

### Topic 7: Behavioral Assessment (4 hours)
- Direct observation
- Self-monitoring
- Functional behavioral analysis
- Behavioral ratings

### Topic 8: Integrative Assessment (8 hours)
- Combining test, interview, behavioral data
- Cross-informant integration
- Differential diagnosis

### Topic 9: Report Writing (10 hours)
- Integrated psychological reports
- Structured vs. narrative
- Feedback to clients
- Communication with referrers

### Topic 10: Cultural and Ethical Issues (4 hours)
- Test bias
- Cultural validity
- DSM-5 cultural formulation
- HK Chinese context

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Psychometrics | Measurement in psychology |
| Reliability | Consistency of measurement |
| Validity | Accuracy of measurement |
| Standardization | Uniform administration procedures |
| Norms | Reference data for comparison |
| Objective test | Standardized scoring |
| Projective test | Ambiguous stimuli for projection |
| MMPI | Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory |
| NEO-PI-R | Revised NEO Personality Inventory |
| DSM-5 | Diagnostic and Statistical Manual, 5th ed. |
| SCID | Structured Clinical Interview for DSM |
| Cultural formulation | Framework for cultural context |
