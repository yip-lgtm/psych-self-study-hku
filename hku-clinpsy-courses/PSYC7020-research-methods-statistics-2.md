# PSYC7020 — Research Methods and Statistics II

## Course Info

- **Code:** PSYC7020
- **Title:** Research methods and statistics II
- **Program:** MSocSc in Clinical Psychology
- **Year:** 1
- **HKU Description:** Continuation of Research Methods and Statistics I. Multivariate methods (MANOVA, multiple regression, factor analysis), mediation and moderation, structural equation modeling, qualitative research methods, mixed methods, and program evaluation.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC7019
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Advanced methods; supports dissertation

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply advanced multivariate methods
2. Conduct mediation and moderation analyses
3. Use structural equation modeling (SEM)
4. Conduct basic qualitative research
5. Design mixed methods studies
6. Evaluate clinical programs
7. Critically evaluate advanced quantitative and qualitative research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Review of Foundation Statistics (3 hours)
- ANOVA, regression
- Effect sizes
- Assumptions

### Topic 2: Multiple Regression Advanced (5 hours)
- Categorical predictors
- Interaction effects
- Multicollinearity
- Hierarchical regression

### Topic 3: Mediation and Moderation (6 hours)
- Baron & Kenny
- Bootstrapping (Hayes PROCESS)
- Conditional process analysis
- Reporting

### Topic 4: MANOVA (3 hours)
- Multivariate analysis of variance
- Discriminant function analysis
- Assumptions

### Topic 5: Factor Analysis (5 hours)
- EFA (Exploratory)
- CFA (Confirmatory)
- Rotation methods
- Reliability (Cronbach's alpha, omega)

### Topic 6: Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) (8 hours)
- Path analysis
- Measurement model
- Structural model
- Fit indices
- Software (lavaan, AMOS)

### Topic 7: Multilevel Modeling (5 hours)
- Nested data
- Random intercepts and slopes
- Software (R lme4, SPSS MIXED)

### Topic 8: Qualitative Methods I (6 hours)
- Phenomenology
- Grounded theory
- IPA (Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis)
- Sampling and saturation

### Topic 9: Qualitative Methods II (6 hours)
- Interviews (semi-structured)
- Focus groups
- Thematic analysis
- NVivo

### Topic 10: Mixed Methods (4 hours)
- Design types (convergent, explanatory, exploratory)
- Integration
- Joint displays

### Topic 11: Program Evaluation (3 hours)
- Logic models
- Process vs. outcome evaluation
- Cost-effectiveness

### Topic 12: Critical Evaluation of Research (3 hours)
- Bias and confounding
- Open science practices
- Pre-registration
- Replication

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Mediation | Indirect effect through mediator |
| Moderation | Interaction effect |
| SEM | Structural equation modeling |
| EFA | Exploratory factor analysis |
| CFA | Confirmatory factor analysis |
| MLM | Multilevel modeling |
| Mixed methods | Combining quantitative and qualitative |
| IPA | Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis |
| Grounded theory | Inductive qualitative method |
| Thematic analysis | Qualitative pattern identification |
| Saturation | No new themes emerging |
| Fit indices | SEM model fit measures (CFI, RMSEA) |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Conduct mediation analysis using Hayes PROCESS or R.
2. Conduct CFA and report fit indices.
3. Conduct thematic analysis on interview data.
4. Design a mixed methods study.
5. Critically evaluate an SEM paper.

## Capstone Connection

Foundation for dissertation statistical analysis. SEM and MLM are common dissertation methods.
