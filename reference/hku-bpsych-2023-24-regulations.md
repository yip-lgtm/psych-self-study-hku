# HKU BPsych 2023-24 Regulations — Key Reference

> **Source:** HKU Faculty of Social Sciences regulations for the degree of Bachelor of Psychology (BPsych), 2023-24 cohort.
> **Why included:** Reference for course equivalence + fallback option if MSocSc(Psy) conversion doesn't fit.

This is the **full undergraduate degree** — 4 years, 240 credits. Different from MSocSc(Psy) (1-year conversion, 8 compulsory courses). You are targeting MSocSc(Psy), but the BPsych regulations are useful because:

1. The MSocSc(Psy) compulsory courses (PSYC7301–7319) are explicitly mapped to BPsych equivalents
2. If you don't get into MSocSc(Psy), you could do BPsych as a 4-year route
3. Understanding BPsych structure helps you write a stronger application

---

## Quick Facts

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4 academic years (8 semesters), full-time |
| **Total credits** | 240 minimum (288 max in normative period; 432 max with extensions) |
| **Major in Psychology** | 84 credits |
| **Major structure** | 6 social sciences pre-req + 48 core + 18 electives + 12 capstone |
| **Off-campus experiential** | 24 credits (Social Innovation + Global Citizenship) |
| **Max registration period** | 6 academic years |
| **Graduation GPA** | ≥ 1.00 to graduate |
| **Honours classification** | 5 divisions (see below) |

---

## Honours Classification (P14)

| Class | GGPA Range |
|---|---|
| First Class Honours | 3.60 – 4.30 |
| Second Class Honours, Division One (2:1) | 3.00 – 3.59 |
| Second Class Honours, Division Two (2:2) | 2.40 – 2.99 |
| Third Class Honours | 1.70 – 2.39 |
| Pass | 1.00 – 1.69 |

- All courses taken (including failed) count toward GGPA, weighted by credit value
- Board of Examiners MAY award a higher class with justification if GGPA is within 0.1 of the next bracket

**Implication for you:** If you ever did BPsych, target First Class (3.60+) for serious PhD applications.

## Course Load Rules

- **Per semester:** 24–30 credits (except summer)
- **Per academic year:** max 72 credits (with permission, more in special cases)
- **First 3 years:** max 108 credits of introductory courses
- **Final year:** NO introductory courses (except Common Core + Chinese)
- **Withdrawal deadline:** 2 weeks after semester start
- **Failed courses:** re-assessment, re-submission, or repeat

---

## Discontinuation Triggers (P9)

The Faculty may discontinue your studies if you:
- Fail ≥ 36 credits in 2 consecutive semesters, OR
- Achieve average Semester GPA < 1.0 in 2 consecutive semesters, OR
- Exceed max 6-year registration

---

## Credit Transfer + Advanced Standing (P11, P12)

- **Advanced standing** granted for studies before admission (not in GPA)
- **Credit transfer** allowed for courses at other institutions (HKU approval needed)
- **Max transfer:** half of total credits required for the degree

**Implication for you:** If you do BPsych later, you can potentially transfer some credits from MSocSc(Psy) — but check the rules carefully.

---

## Assessment (P6, P7, P8)

- Continuous assessment + written exams + other activities
- **Cannot repeat** a course for upgrade if already passed with D or above
- **No appeal** against assessment results

---

## Experiential Learning (P4h)

- 24 credits of off-campus experiential learning
- Includes Social Innovation + Global Citizenship requirements
- For BPsych students only — not relevant to MSocSc(Psy) conversion

---

## Key Takeaways for Your Plan

1. **Stay on the MSocSc(Psy) path** unless the conversion route genuinely doesn't work — it's 1 year vs 4 years
2. **If you ever need BPsych:** 4 years full-time, target First Class (3.60+) for PhD
3. **Capstone in BPsych is 12 credits** — your self-study capstone is good practice for this format
4. **Honours classification matters** for PhD applications (most programs want at least 2:1)
5. **Credit transfer is limited** — don't expect your self-study to "count" for credit

## When BPsych Might Be Right (and When It Isn't)

### Choose BPsych if:
- MSocSc(Psy) doesn't accept you (highly unlikely with this self-study plan)
- You want a deeper foundation + undergraduate peer cohort
- You have 4 years to invest and the financial runway
- You're considering a future academic career (PhD → lecturer track)

### Stay on MSocSc(Psy) if:
- You want to minimize time/cost (1 year vs 4)
- You're already self-disciplined and don't need the structure
- You have clear subfield direction and a research agenda ready
- You want to start the "real" psychology training (postgrad) ASAP

### Hybrid consideration:
- Do MSocSc(Psy) (1 year) → MPhil/PhD in Psychology (2–4 years)
- This is the most efficient path to a research career
- BPsych is mostly a fallback if MSocSc(Psy) is denied
