# PSYC2051 — Perception

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2051
- **Title:** Perception
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** An introduction to sensation and perception, with an emphasis on the psychology of seeing. Specific topics include the following: examination of the functional properties of sensory systems (e.g., auditory system, color vision, vestibular system, touch and kinaesthesia); phenomenology of sensation and perception; psychophysical limits of perceptual systems; goals of sensory coding; structure and evolution of sensory systems; theories of perception. Perceptual experiments will be conducted by students in laboratory classes.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Lab component; psychophysics

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major sensory systems
2. Apply psychophysical methods
3. Distinguish sensation and perception
4. Discuss theories of perception
5. Conduct perceptual experiments
6. Analyze psychophysical data
7. Discuss evolutionary aspects of perception

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction (3 hours)
- Sensation vs. perception
- Bottom-up vs. top-down processing
- Direct vs. indirect perception

### Topic 2: Visual System (5 hours)
- Eye anatomy
- Retina and photoreceptors
- Visual pathways
- Visual cortex
- Color vision (trichromatic and opponent-process)

### Topic 3: Auditory System (4 hours)
- Ear anatomy
- Frequency and pitch
- Intensity and loudness
- Sound localization

### Topic 4: Other Senses (4 hours)
- Touch and kinesthesis
- Vestibular system
- Taste and smell
- Multimodal perception

### Topic 5: Psychophysics (5 hours)
- Absolute threshold
- Difference threshold (JND)
- Weber's law
- Fechner's law
- Stevens' power law
- Signal detection theory

### Topic 6: Perceptual Organization (5 hours)
- Gestalt principles
- Figure-ground
- Depth perception (binocular and monocular cues)
- Motion perception
- Perceptual constancies

### Topic 7: Attention and Perception (3 hours)
- Selective attention
- Inattentional blindness
- Change blindness
- Perceptual load theory

### Topic 8: Theories of Perception (4 hours)
- Direct perception (Gibson)
- Constructivist (Gregory)
- Bayesian brain
- Computational approaches

### Topic 9: Perceptual Learning and Development (3 hours)
- Critical periods (e.g., vision)
- Perceptual adaptation
- Cross-modal plasticity

### Topic 10: Applied Perception (3 hours)
- Visual illusions
- Art and design
- Ergonomics
- Disorders (agnosia, blindsight)

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Sensation | Detection of physical stimulus |
| Perception | Interpretation of sensation |
| Psychophysics | Quantitative study of sensation-perception relationship |
| Absolute threshold | Minimum detectable stimulus |
| JND | Just noticeable difference |
| Weber's law | JND proportional to stimulus magnitude |
| Signal detection theory | Separates signal from noise |
| Bottom-up | Data-driven processing |
| Top-down | Concept-driven processing |
| Gestalt | Whole greater than sum of parts |
| Trichromatic theory | Three types of cones for color |
| Opponent-process | Color processed in pairs |
| Agnosia | Inability to recognize objects |
