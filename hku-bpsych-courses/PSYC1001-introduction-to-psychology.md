# PSYC1001 — Introduction to Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC1001
- **Title:** Introduction to psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** Discussion of basic concepts in psychology and a preliminary survey of representative work carried out in various areas of psychological investigation, together with an investigation at some length of one such area.
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Foundational course; required for all higher-level psych courses

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe the major subfields of psychology and their key questions
2. Apply the scientific method to behavior and mental processes
3. Identify the major theoretical perspectives (biological, behavioral, cognitive, psychodynamic, humanistic)
4. Critically evaluate psychological claims in everyday life
5. Read and summarize primary research in psychology
6. Conduct a deeper investigation into one subfield of interest

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: What is Psychology? (3 hours)
- Definition and scope
- Major perspectives: biological, behavioral, cognitive, psychodynamic, humanistic
- History: Wundt, James, behaviorism, cognitive revolution
- Modern psychology as a science

### Topic 2: Research Methods in Psychology (5 hours)
- Scientific method
- Experimental, correlational, observational, survey methods
- Variables, sampling, ethics
- Reading research papers (3-pass method)

### Topic 3: Biological Bases of Behavior (4 hours)
- Neurons and brain structure
- Neurotransmitters
- Brain-behavior relationships
- Methods: EEG, fMRI, TMS

### Topic 4: Sensation and Perception (3 hours)
- Sensory systems
- Gestalt principles
- Perceptual organization
- Illusions

### Topic 5: Learning (4 hours)
- Classical conditioning (Pavlov)
- Operant conditioning (Skinner)
- Observational learning (Bandura)
- Cognitive learning

### Topic 6: Memory (3 hours)
- Encoding, storage, retrieval
- Working memory, long-term memory
- Forgetting and distortion

### Topic 7: Cognition, Language, and Thought (3 hours)
- Problem solving
- Decision making and heuristics
- Language acquisition and processing
- Bilingualism

### Topic 8: Development Across the Lifespan (3 hours)
- Piaget, Vygotsky, attachment
- Cognitive, social, emotional development
- Aging

### Topic 9: Social and Personality (3 hours)
- Social cognition and influence
- Personality theories
- Self and identity

### Topic 10: Mental Health and Applied Psychology (3 hours)
- Major categories of disorder
- Therapy approaches
- Applications: health, forensic, organizational, sport

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Behaviorism | Approach focusing on observable behavior (Watson, Skinner) |
| Cognitive revolution | 1950s–60s shift from behaviorism to mental processes |
| Nativism | View that certain abilities are innate (Chomsky) |
| Empiricism | View that knowledge comes from experience |
| Hypothesis | Testable prediction |
| Theory | Integrated set of principles explaining phenomena |
| Replicate | Repeat a study to verify findings |

## Self-Check Questions

1. What are the 7 major perspectives in psychology? Give a one-sentence description of each.
2. Explain the difference between classical and operant conditioning. Give a real-world example of each.
3. What is the replication crisis? Why is it relevant to psychology?
4. Describe Piaget's stages of cognitive development.
5. Compare the biological and behavioral perspectives on a specific behavior (e.g., aggression).

**Tip:** Use the "investigation at some length of one such area" component of this course to start exploring your capstone subfield.
