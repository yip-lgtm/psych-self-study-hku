# PSYC2059 — Current Issues in Applied Developmental Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2059
- **Title:** Current issues in applied developmental psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course will explore applications of developmental psychology to issues pertaining to improving the well-being of children and their families. Topics include: The quality of child care, patterns and ranges of normal child behaviors, child-rearing practices, developmentally challenged children, legal and public-policy issues.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Applied focus; policy perspective

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply developmental psychology to real-world child and family issues
2. Evaluate child care quality
3. Discuss normal vs. atypical child development
4. Analyze child-rearing practices
5. Discuss developmental disabilities
6. Apply developmental science to legal and policy issues

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Applied Developmental Psychology (3 hours)
- From theory to practice
- Evidence-based interventions
- Ethical considerations

### Topic 2: Child Care Quality (4 hours)
- Quality indicators (structural, process)
- Home vs. center care
- Effects on development
- Cost-quality tradeoffs

### Topic 3: Normal Child Behavior (3 hours)
- Tantrums, sleep, eating
- Fears and anxieties
- Sibling relationships
- Cultural variations in "normal"

### Topic 4: Child-Rearing Practices (4 hours)
- Parenting styles (Baumrind)
- Authoritative vs. authoritarian vs. permissive
- Cross-cultural parenting
- Discipline approaches

### Topic 5: Attachment-Based Interventions (3 hours)
- Circle of Security
- Child-Parent Psychotherapy
- Attachment and Biobehavioral Catch-up
- Evidence base

### Topic 6: Developmental Disabilities (5 hours)
- Intellectual disability
- Autism Spectrum Disorder
- ADHD
- Specific learning disabilities
- Early intervention
- Family impact

### Topic 7: Risk and Resilience (4 hours)
- Risk factors (poverty, maltreatment, parental mental illness)
- Resilience processes
- Intervention programs
- Cultural resilience

### Topic 8: Legal Issues (4 hours)
- Child custody
- Child testimony
- Mandatory reporting
- Adoption and foster care
- Juvenile justice

### Topic 9: Public Policy (3 hours)
- Early childhood education (Head Start, HK context)
- Family leave policies
- Child welfare systems
- Cross-national comparisons

### Topic 10: Contemporary Debates (3 hours)
- Screen time and development
- Social media effects
- COVID-19 impact on children
- Climate anxiety

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Applied developmental psych | Use of developmental science to solve real problems |
| Authoritative parenting | High warmth, high structure |
| Developmental disability | Condition affecting development |
| Resilience | Positive adaptation despite adversity |
| Early intervention | Services for young children with delays |
| Custody evaluation | Psychological assessment for court |
| Foster care | Out-of-home placement |
| Adoption | Legal transfer of parental rights |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare parenting styles. Which is associated with best outcomes?
2. What is "toxic stress"? How does it affect development?
3. Discuss the role of attachment in intervention programs.
4. What are the challenges of assessing children's testimony?
5. How would you design an early intervention program in HK?
