# PSYC3061 — Advanced Issues in Perception

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3061
- **Title:** Advanced issues in perception
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced seminar course reviews findings from both recent and classical research on human perceptual systems. Modules will consider in-depth, select special topics such as cross-modal perceptual interactions, lessons from abnormal perception in agnosia, amblyopia, etc. Modules will be discussed from a multidisciplinary standpoint, integrating computational, psychophysical and neurobiological approaches. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology and neuroscience.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and PSYC2051
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Seminar; multidisciplinary; advanced

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate recent and classical perception research
2. Discuss cross-modal perceptual interactions
3. Apply findings from abnormal perception
4. Integrate computational, psychophysical, and neurobiological approaches
5. Conduct advanced perception research
6. Discuss current debates in perception

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Current Debates in Perception (4 hours)
- Predictive coding
- Bayesian brain
- Embodied perception
- Active inference

### Topic 2: Multisensory Integration (5 hours)
- Visual-auditory interactions
- Visual-tactile interactions
- McGurk effect
- Ventriloquism effect
- Bayesian models of integration

### Topic 3: Abnormal Perception I — Agnosias (4 hours)
- Visual agnosia
- Auditory agnosia
- Tactile agnosia
- Prosopagnosia
- What they reveal about normal perception

### Topic 4: Abnormal Perception II — Other Conditions (4 hours)
- Synesthesia
- Blindness and cross-modal plasticity
- Amnesia and perception
- Williams syndrome
- Autism and perception

### Topic 5: Computational Approaches (5 hours)
- Deep learning models of vision
- Predictive processing
- Hierarchical Bayesian models
- Comparison with biological vision

### Topic 6: Psychophysical Methods (4 hours)
- Adaptive staircases
- Signal detection theory
- Classification images
- Reverse correlation

### Topic 7: Neurobiological Approaches (4 hours)
- Visual cortex organization
- Attention and perception
- Decision-making and perception
- Recent imaging studies

### Topic 8: Active Perception (3 hours)
- Eye movements and perception
- Touch and perception
- Multisensory action
- Active inference

### Topic 9: Consciousness and Perception (3 hours)
- Hard problem of consciousness
- Visual awareness
- Neural correlates of perception
- Theories of consciousness

### Topic 10: Research Project (8 hours)
- Independent or small group
- Original data collection or analysis
- Class presentation

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Predictive coding | Brain generates and updates predictions |
| Bayesian brain | Perception as Bayesian inference |
| Embodied perception | Body shapes perception |
| Active inference | Action as inference |
| Multisensory integration | Combining sensory inputs |
| Agnosia | Loss of recognition |
| Synesthesia | Cross-sensory perception |
| McGurk effect | Visual-auditory speech integration |
| Ventriloquism effect | Sound attributed to visual source |
| Psychophysics | Quantitative measurement of perception |
| Signal detection theory | Discriminating signal from noise |

## Self-Check Questions

4. Discuss deep learning as a model of human vision.
5. What is the relationship between perception and consciousness?
