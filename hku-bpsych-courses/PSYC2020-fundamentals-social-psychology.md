# PSYC2020 — Fundamentals of Social Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2020
- **Title:** Fundamentals of social psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** The course gives an overview of the field which studies the behaviour of individuals in social contexts. It covers social perception, social cognition, social motivation, attitudes and attitude change, relationship between attitude and behaviour, aggression, helping, interpersonal attraction, social influence on individual behaviour and group dynamics. The impact of Chinese culture on various social behaviours will form part of the discussion.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Includes Chinese culture perspective on social behavior

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major social psychological processes
2. Discuss social perception, cognition, and influence
3. Apply social psychology to real-world issues
4. Discuss cultural influences on social behavior
5. Critically evaluate social psychology research
6. Conduct social psychology research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction (3 hours)
- Definition and scope
- Research methods
- Chinese cultural context

### Topic 2: Social Perception (4 hours)
- Attribution theory
- Fundamental attribution error
- Self-serving bias
- Impression formation

### Topic 3: Social Cognition (4 hours)
- Schemas
- Heuristics
- Automatic vs. controlled processing
- Stereotypes and prejudice

### Topic 4: Attitudes and Persuasion (4 hours)
- Attitude structure and function
- Cognitive dissonance
- Elaboration Likelihood Model
- Attitude-behavior consistency

### Topic 5: Social Motivation (3 hours)
- Need for achievement, affiliation, power
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation
- Self-determination theory

### Topic 6: Aggression (3 hours)
- Biological and social factors
- Frustration-aggression hypothesis
- Media violence
- Cultural variations (Chinese context)

### Topic 7: Prosocial Behavior (3 hours)
- Bystander effect
- Empathy-altruism hypothesis
- Social norms
- Cultural influences on helping

### Topic 8: Interpersonal Attraction (3 hours)
- Proximity, similarity, attractiveness
- Attachment styles in adults
- Chinese relationship patterns

### Topic 9: Social Influence (4 hours)
- Conformity (Asch)
- Obedience (Milgram)
- Compliance techniques
- Cultural differences in conformity

### Topic 10: Group Dynamics (4 hours)
- Group polarization
- Groupthink
- Social facilitation and loafing
- Bystander effect in groups

### Topic 11: Chinese Cultural Context (3 hours)
- Confucian influences (face, harmony)
- Collectivism vs. individualism
- Cross-cultural comparisons
- Indigenous Chinese social psychology

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Attribution | Process of explaining behavior |
| Fundamental attribution error | Overemphasize disposition for others |
| Cognitive dissonance | Discomfort from inconsistent cognitions |
| Conformity | Adjusting to group norms |
| Obedience | Compliance with authority |
| Groupthink | Poor decisions from desire for harmony |
| Bystander effect | Others reduce helping |
| Self-serving bias | Credit self, blame situation |
| Face (面子) | Social standing in Chinese culture |
| Guanxi (關係) | Personal connections in Chinese culture |

## Self-Check Questions
