# PSYC2063 — Industrial/Organizational Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2063
- **Title:** Industrial/organizational psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course surveys the complex relationships that exist between people, their social environment, and their work. Topics may include job analysis, personnel selection, personnel training, performance management, vocational guidance, work motivation, job satisfaction, leadership, group relations, conflicts, organization design, occupational stress, and errors.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Direct application to your engineering work context

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply psychology to workplace issues
2. Conduct job analyses
3. Evaluate personnel selection methods
4. Design training programs
5. Discuss work motivation and job satisfaction
6. Analyze leadership and group dynamics
7. Address occupational stress and errors
8. Apply I-O psychology to engineering contexts

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to I-O Psychology (3 hours)
- Definition and scope
- History (Taylor, Hawthorne, WWII)
- Research methods
- Professional ethics (SIOP)

### Topic 2: Job Analysis (4 hours)
- Methods (O*NET, interviews, observation)
- Job descriptions vs. specifications
- Competency modeling
- HK construction industry context

### Topic 3: Personnel Selection (5 hours)
- Recruitment
- Application blanks, references
- Interviews (structured vs. unstructured)
- Tests (cognitive, personality, integrity)
- Work samples, assessment centers
- Validity and adverse impact

### Topic 4: Performance Management (4 hours)
- Performance appraisal methods
- 360-degree feedback
- Rating errors (halo, leniency, central tendency)
- Goal setting (Locke & Latham)

### Topic 5: Training and Development (4 hours)
- Needs assessment
- Training design
- Delivery methods
- Evaluation (Kirkpatrick model)
- Transfer of training

### Topic 6: Work Motivation (5 hours)
- Maslow, Herzberg, McGregor
- Self-determination theory
- Expectancy theory (Vroom)
- Equity theory (Adams)
- Job characteristics model (Hackman & Oldham)
- Cross-cultural motivation

### Topic 7: Job Satisfaction and Engagement (3 hours)
- Measurement
- Antecedents and consequences
- Job attitudes
- Employee engagement
- Turnover

### Topic 8: Leadership (4 hours)
- Trait approaches
- Behavioral (Ohio State, Michigan)
- Contingency (Fiedler)
- Transformational vs. transactional
- Authentic leadership
- Cross-cultural leadership

### Topic 9: Group Dynamics in Organizations (3 hours)
- Team effectiveness
- Group development (Tuckman)
- Conflict management
- Negotiation

### Topic 10: Occupational Stress and Well-being (4 hours)
- Sources of stress
- Job demands-resources model
- Burnout
- Work-life balance
- Interventions

### Topic 11: Human Factors and Errors (3 hours)
- Human error types (slips, mistakes, violations)
- Safety culture
- High-reliability organizations
- Engineering psychology

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| I-O psychology | Industrial-organizational psychology |
| Job analysis | Systematic study of job content |
| Personnel selection | Choosing among job candidates |
| Reliability (psychometric) | Consistency of measurement |
| Validity (psychometric) | Accuracy of measurement |
| Adverse impact | Disproportionate negative effect on protected group |
| 360-degree feedback | Feedback from multiple sources |
| Self-determination theory | Motivation through autonomy, competence, relatedness |
| Hawthorne effect | Behavior change from being observed |
| Job demands-resources | Work stress model |
| Burnout | Exhaustion from chronic work stress |
| Transformational leadership | Inspiring and empowering |
