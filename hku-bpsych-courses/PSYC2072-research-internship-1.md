# PSYC2072 — Research Internship in Psychology I

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2072
- **Title:** Research internship in psychology I
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** Students will have an opportunity to learn to do research as an intern in ongoing empirical research projects under a teacher's supervision in the Department of Psychology. Students spend 10-12 hours per week assisting various research activities. The internship includes participating in lab meetings or meeting individually with the supervisor, reading relevant theoretical and empirical articles, assisting in ongoing empirical research projects, and writing an internship report. Information about research projects offering internship placements and application procedure will be available in the Psychology Department webpage. Internship I and II can be done with the same supervisor or two different supervisors.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** 10-12 hours/week lab work; intern under faculty; highly recommended for grad school

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Participate in active psychological research
2. Apply classroom knowledge to real research projects
3. Develop specific research skills (coding, data collection, analysis)
4. Read and synthesize research literature
5. Communicate research findings in oral and written form
6. Build a mentoring relationship with a faculty member
7. Make an informed decision about research career

## Topic Outline

**The specific activities depend on the lab/supervisor. The following is a representative structure.**

### Topic 1: Lab Onboarding (5-10 hours)
- Lab expectations and norms
- Lab safety (if applicable)
- IRB / ethics training
- Equipment/software setup
- Meeting lab members

### Topic 2: Background Reading (15-25 hours)
- Foundational papers from the lab
- Recent publications
- Method papers
- Synthesis and discussion

### Topic 3: Research Activity — Data Collection (30-50 hours)
- Recruiting participants
- Running sessions (if experimental)
- Coding data
- Lab notebook maintenance

### Topic 4: Research Activity — Data Analysis (20-30 hours)
- Cleaning data
- Running analyses
- Generating figures
- Learning lab's analysis pipeline

### Topic 5: Lab Meetings (weekly, ongoing)
- Present research updates
- Discuss recent papers
- Brainstorm
- Get feedback

### Topic 6: Independent Project (15-25 hours)
- Depending on lab, may lead to a small independent study
- Or contribute to ongoing project

### Topic 7: Internship Report (15-20 hours)
- 10-15 page report
- Background, methods, results (if applicable), reflection
- APA format

### Topic 8: Final Presentation (5-10 hours)
- Oral or poster presentation
- Lab and possibly department

## Required Resources

**Varies by lab.** Generally:
- Lab manual / protocols
- Specific software (R, Python, PsychoPy, MATLAB, etc.)
- Access to lab databases

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Lab meeting | Regular group discussion of research |
| IRB | Institutional Review Board (ethics) |
| Informed consent | Process of explaining study to participants |
| Data management | Organization and storage of research data |
| Pre-registration | Publicly registering hypotheses before data collection |
| Replication | Repeating a study to verify findings |
| Open science | Practices making research more transparent |

## Self-Check Questions

1. What did you learn about the research process in this internship?
2. What specific skills did you develop?
3. How does this experience inform your career goals?
4. What were the challenges? How did you address them?
5. How will you build on this experience going forward?

## Capstone Connection

**Highly relevant** — research internship experience is:
- The best preparation for your own empirical project
- A strong signal for grad school applications
- An opportunity to develop faculty relationships (for references)

**Tip:** Choose a lab aligned with your capstone subfield. Skills you learn (analysis, methods) transfer directly.

**For your self-study:** Even without a formal lab, you can replicate this experience by:
- Volunteering to help with a faculty researcher's project (offer free analysis work)
- Doing an independent replication of a published study
- Joining online research consortia
