# PSYC2010 — History and Issues in Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2010
- **Title:** History and issues in psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course provides an in-depth study of psychology within an historically- and issue-based framework. Beginning with the philosophical and physiological forerunners to how the modern discipline became an experimental, scientific project, it will move on to examine some of the debates in the West in the first half of the twentieth century which either forestalled or encouraged the use of mental concepts and the (re)emergence of cognitive psychology. The course will end by a discussion over how psychology in the West was brought into the East and various issues arising from such a history.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Includes East-West / HK-specific perspective

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Trace the philosophical and physiological roots of modern psychology
2. Describe the major "schools" of psychology and their debates
3. Explain the cognitive revolution and its causes
4. Discuss how psychology spread from West to East
5. Critically evaluate the field's historical assumptions
6. Apply historical understanding to current issues
7. Discuss culturally-specific issues in psychology (esp. Hong Kong)

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Philosophical Roots (4 hours)
- Ancient Greek philosophy (Plato, Aristotle)
- Rationalism vs. empiricism (Descartes, Locke, Hume)
- Kant and the synthetic a priori
- Philosophy of mind

### Topic 2: Physiological Foundations (3 hours)
- Early neuroanatomy
- Phrenology
- Bell-Magendie law
- Helmholtz and psychophysics

### Topic 3: Founding of Experimental Psychology (4 hours)
- Wilhelm Wundt and Leipzig
- Structuralism (Titchener)
- Functionalism (James, Dewey)
- Early research methods

### Topic 4: Early 20th Century Schools (5 hours)
- Psychoanalysis (Freud, Jung, Adler)
- Behaviorism (Watson, Skinner)
- Gestalt psychology (Wertheimer, Köhler)
- Humanistic psychology (Rogers, Maslow)

### Topic 5: The Crisis and the Cognitive Revolution (4 hours)
- Behaviorism's dominance
- Chomsky's review of Skinner (1959)
- Miller, Bruner, and the cognitive revolution
- Information processing as paradigm

### Topic 6: Post-War Psychology (3 hours)
- WWII and applied psychology
- Clinical psychology's growth
- Industrial/organizational psychology
- Community psychology

### Topic 7: Psychology Spreads to East Asia (4 hours)
- Japan: Meiji era to post-war
- China: 1900s, Mao era, reform
- Hong Kong: British colonial influence, post-1997
- Indigenous psychologies (Hofstede, Chinese psychology)

### Topic 8: Contemporary Debates (4 hours)
- Nature vs. nurture
- Reductionism vs. holism
- Idiographic vs. nomothetic
- Replication crisis
- Open science movement

### Topic 9: Cultural Issues in Psychology (3 hours)
- WEIRD samples (Henrich et al., 2010)
- Individualism vs. collectivism
- Indigenous psychologies
- Decolonizing psychology

### Topic 10: The Future of Psychology (4 hours)
- AI and computational modeling
- Big data and digital phenotyping
- Cross-cultural collaboration
- Global mental health

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Empiricism | Knowledge from sensory experience |
| Nativism | Knowledge is innate |
| Structuralism | Study of mental elements |
| Functionalism | Study of function of consciousness |
| Behaviorism | Only study observable behavior |
| Gestalt | Whole is greater than sum of parts |
| Cognitive revolution | 1950s-60s shift to mental processes |
| WEIRD | Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare rationalism and empiricism. How did each influence psychology?
2. Why was Chomsky's 1959 review of Skinner so influential?
3. Describe the cognitive revolution. What were its causes?
4. Discuss how psychology developed differently in East Asia vs. the West.
5. What is the replication crisis? How is it changing the field?
