# PSYC3052 — Advanced Social Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3052
- **Title:** Advanced social psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced laboratory course is designed for students interested in learning how to conduct studies in social psychology. Students will develop skills in critically evaluating current theoretical controversies and methodological paradigms. Special attention is given to theoretical, methodological, and measurement issues such as critical thinking in social psychology, social research design, proposal writing, and research ethics. This course is conducted in a seminar format with the expectation that students will participate actively and on occasion help lead a discussion. Some combination of readings, written assignments, and oral presentation is required. Students will each do an independent empirical research project. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and either PSYC2019 or PSYC2020
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Lab course; independent empirical project required
- **Mutually exclusive with:** PSYC3051

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Conduct independent empirical social psychology research
2. Design and execute studies
3. Critically evaluate social psychology theories
4. Apply advanced research methods
5. Write a research proposal and report
6. Discuss research ethics in social psych
7. Communicate research findings

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Current Theoretical Controversies (5 hours)
- Replication crisis in social psychology
- Power posing (and failure to replicate)
- Facial feedback hypothesis
- Stereotype threat debate
- Embodied cognition

### Topic 2: Research Design (5 hours)
- Experimental design
- Field experiments
- Ecological validity
- Statistical power
- Pre-registration

### Topic 3: Measurement in Social Psych (5 hours)
- Self-report measures
- Behavioral measures
- Implicit measures
- Physiological measures
- Social network analysis

### Topic 4: Special Methodological Issues (4 hours)
- Demand characteristics
- Experimenter effects
- WeDemand characteristics
- Cultural moderators
- Online data collection

### Topic 5: Proposal Writing (5 hours)
- Literature review
- Research question
- Hypotheses
- Method
- Analysis plan
- Pilot data

### Topic 6: Conducting Research (10 hours)
- IRB approval
- Recruitment
- Data collection
- Troubleshooting
- Lab notebook

### Topic 7: Analyzing Data (5 hours)
- ANOVA / regression
- Moderation / mediation
- Multilevel modeling
- Bayesian alternatives
- Effect sizes

### Topic 8: Writing the Report (5 hours)
- APA 7th format
- Results section
- Discussion
- Limitations
- Future directions

### Topic 9: Research Ethics (3 hours)
- IRB process
- Informed consent
- Deception
- Debriefing
- Data sharing

### Topic 10: Student Presentations (4 hours)
- Final project presentations
- Peer feedback
- Discussion leadership

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Social psychology | Scientific study of social behavior |
| Replication crisis | Failure to replicate many findings |
| Pre-registration | Publicly registering hypotheses |
| Effect size | Standardized magnitude |
| Demand characteristics | Cues revealing study purpose |
| Experimenter effects | Researcher influence on outcomes |
| Social desirability | Tendency to give favorable responses |
| Implicit measure | Indirect measure of attitude |
| Field experiment | Study in natural setting |
| Ecological validity | Generalizability to real life |

## Self-Check Questions

2. What is pre-registration? How does it address the crisis?
3. Compare experimental and field approaches.
4. Design a study to test a social psychology hypothesis.
5. What are the ethical issues in social psych research using deception?
