# PSYC2111 — Neurobiological Basis of Psychological Issues

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2111
- **Title:** Neurobiological basis of psychological issues
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** Human behavior is generated by complex psychophysiological mechanisms of the brain. This course is designed to provide a broad introduction to the biological basis of stress, emotion, and regulation of cognitive-affective processes affecting psychological health. The neurobiological basis of psychopathologies e.g. depression, anxiety, will be examined as examples to demonstrate the complex relationships between brain, behavior, and psychopathology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Bridge between neuroscience and psychopathology

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe neurobiology of stress
2. Explain neural circuits of emotion
3. Discuss regulation of cognitive-affective processes
4. Apply neurobiological knowledge to psychopathology
5. Connect brain, behavior, and mental health
6. Critically evaluate psychopharmacology

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction (3 hours)
- Brain-behavior relationship
- Reductionism in psychopathology
- Translational neuroscience

### Topic 2: Neurobiology of Stress (5 hours)
- HPA axis
- Cortisol and allostasis
- Chronic stress and brain
- Early life stress
- Resilience factors

### Topic 3: Neurobiology of Emotion (5 hours)
- Limbic system
- Amygdala and fear
- Prefrontal regulation
- Emotional memory
- Individual differences

### Topic 4: Serotonin and Depression (4 hours)
- Monoamine hypothesis
- SSRI mechanism
- Limits of the chemical imbalance theory
- Neurogenesis hypothesis
- Inflammation hypothesis

### Topic 5: Dopamine and Reward (3 hours)
- Mesolimbic pathway
- Reward prediction error
- Anhedonia
- Substance use disorders
- Behavioral addiction

### Topic 6: GABA and Anxiety (3 hours)
- GABAergic inhibition
- Benzodiazepines
- Anxiolytics
- Fear conditioning
- Generalized anxiety

### Topic 7: Glutamate and Schizophrenia (3 hours)
- NMDA receptor hypothesis
- Psychotomimetic effects
- Cognitive deficits
- Treatment implications

### Topic 8: Neurobiology of Trauma (3 hours)
- Fear circuitry
- Stress sensitization
- PTSD
- HPA dysregulation
- Treatment approaches

### Topic 9: Neuroinflammation and Mental Health (3 hours)
- Microglia and neuroinflammation
- Cytokines and depression
- Gut-brain axis
- Implications for treatment

### Topic 10: Psychopharmacology Overview (4 hours)
- Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs)
- Anxiolytics (benzos, buspirone)
- Antipsychotics (typical, atypical)
- Mood stabilizers (lithium, valproate)
- Stimulants
- Ethical issues
