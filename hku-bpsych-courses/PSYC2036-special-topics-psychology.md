# PSYC2036 — Special Topics in Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2036
- **Title:** Special topics in psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course provides an opportunity to study in some depth an area of psychology of interest to students and a staff member alike. Individual topics may have special requirements for eligibility. Students taking this course may select one topic from a list of topics to be announced in the semester immediately prior to that in which they are taken.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Topic varies by semester; check department announcement

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Demonstrate deep understanding of a specific psychological topic
2. Critically evaluate research in that topic
3. Synthesize information from multiple sources
4. Apply knowledge to novel situations
5. Communicate findings effectively in written and oral form

## Topic Outline

**The specific topics covered vary by semester. This file is a template for studying ANY special topic course.**

### Topic 1: Topic Identification (3 hours)
- Review the announced topic for the current semester
- Identify key themes, questions, debates
- Find the key papers and authors

### Topic 2: Literature Search Strategy (3 hours)
- Database search (PsycINFO, Google Scholar)
- Identify seminal and recent papers
- Snowball from key papers
- Build a reading list

### Topic 3: Foundational Reading (5 hours)
- Read 3-5 seminal papers with 3-pass method
- Note key concepts, methods, findings
- Identify major theoretical frameworks

### Topic 4: Contemporary Research (5 hours)
- Read recent (last 5 years) papers
- Note current debates
- Identify gaps in the literature

### Topic 5: Methodological Approaches (3 hours)
- What methods are typical in this area?
- Strengths and limitations
- How would you extend the research?

### Topic 6: Critical Analysis (3 hours)
- Compare and contrast theories
- Evaluate evidence
- Form your own assessment

### Topic 7: Application (3 hours)
- How does this topic apply to real-world problems?
- Connect to your capstone interests
- Examples from practice

### Topic 8: Synthesis and Communication (3 hours)
- Write a critical review
- Prepare an oral presentation
- Peer feedback
