# PSYC2009 — Life-Span Developmental Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2009
- **Title:** Life-span developmental psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course provides an introduction to developmental psychology from a life-span perspective. The topics include: basic concepts and theories of human development; research methodology and issues in the study of developmental change; biological, environmental and social influences on development; processes of physical development over the life-span; attachment and emotional development; development of perception, language, cognition and morality; development of personality and social relationships.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major theories of lifespan development
2. Discuss research methods in developmental psychology
3. Explain physical, cognitive, and socioemotional development across the lifespan
4. Apply attachment theory to relationships
5. Discuss the role of nature, nurture, and their interaction
6. Critically evaluate developmental research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Lifespan Development (3 hours)
- Lifespan perspective
- Nature vs. nurture (interactionist view)
- Continuity vs. discontinuity
- Critical vs. sensitive periods
- Research methods (longitudinal, cross-sectional, sequential)

### Topic 2: Theories of Development (5 hours)
- Piaget (cognitive stages)
- Vygotsky (sociocultural)
- Erikson (psychosocial)
- Bowlby/Ainsworth (attachment)
- Bronfenbrenner (ecological systems)

### Topic 3: Prenatal Development and Birth (3 hours)
- Germinal, embryonic, fetal stages
- Teratogens
- Birth complications
- Newborn capacities

### Topic 4: Infancy (5 hours)
- Physical and motor development
- Sensory and perceptual development
- Cognitive development
- Language development
- Temperament

### Topic 5: Attachment (4 hours)
- Bowlby's ethological theory
- Ainsworth's Strange Situation
- Attachment patterns (secure, avoidant, ambivalent, disorganized)
- Internal working models
- Cross-cultural variations

### Topic 6: Early and Middle Childhood (5 hours)
- Physical development
- Cognitive development (Piaget, Vygotsky)
- Language and literacy
- Moral development (Kohlberg, Gilligan)
- Peer relationships

### Topic 7: Adolescence (4 hours)
- Puberty
- Cognitive changes (formal operational)
- Identity formation (Erikson)
- Risk-taking
- Peer influence

### Topic 8: Early and Middle Adulthood (4 hours)
- Physical changes
- Career and work
- Relationships
- Parenthood
- Midlife

### Topic 9: Late Adulthood and Aging (4 hours)
- Physical and cognitive changes
- Socioemotional selectivity
- Dementia
- Successful aging
- Death and dying

### Topic 10: Research Methods and Contemporary Issues (3 hours)
- Cross-cultural perspectives
- Technology and development
- Applied developmental psychology
- Policy implications

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Schema | Mental framework |
| Assimilation | Incorporating new info into existing schema |
| Accommodation | Modifying schema for new info |
| ZPD | Zone of proximal development |
| Attachment | Lasting bond between infant and caregiver |
| Secure base | Caregiver as base for exploration |
| Theory of mind | Understanding others have thoughts |
| Critical period | Time window for development |
| Plasticity | Capacity for change |
