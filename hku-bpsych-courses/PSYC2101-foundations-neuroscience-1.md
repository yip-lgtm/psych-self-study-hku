# PSYC2101 — Foundations of Neuroscience I

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2101
- **Title:** Foundations of neuroscience I
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course covers the fundamental principles of neuroscience. Topics include history of neuroscience, neurons and glia, neuronal membrane at rest, action potential, synaptic transmission, neuroanatomy, the somatic sensory system, chemical senses: taste and smell. the auditory system, vision and the eye, vision and the brain, spinal control of movement, brain control of movement, chemical control of the brain and behavior, development in the nervous system, memory systems, learning and memory: molecular biology, emotion and attention. (Priority will be given to students planning to major in neuroscience)
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Foundational for neuroscience major; prereq for PSYC2102, 2103, 2110

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe the cellular basis of neural function
2. Explain synaptic transmission
3. Identify major neuroanatomical structures
4. Discuss sensory systems
5. Explain motor control
6. Describe neural development
7. Discuss memory systems
8. Connect cellular to systems neuroscience

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: History of Neuroscience (2 hours)
- Ancient views
- Renaissance (Vesalius, Descartes)
- Modern era (Cajal, Sherrington, Hubel & Wiesel)
- HK neuroscience context

### Topic 2: Cells of the Nervous System (4 hours)
- Neuron types
- Glial cells (astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia)
- Blood-brain barrier

### Topic 3: Neuronal Membrane at Rest (3 hours)
- Membrane potential
- Ion channels
- Nernst equation
- Goldman equation

### Topic 4: Action Potential (4 hours)
- Threshold
- Depolarization, repolarization
- Ion channels (Na+, K+)
- Refractory periods
- Saltatory conduction

### Topic 5: Synaptic Transmission (4 hours)
- Electrical vs. chemical synapses
- Neurotransmitter release
- Postsynaptic receptors
- EPSPs, IPSPs
- Integration

### Topic 6: Neuroanatomy Overview (4 hours)
- CNS vs. PNS
- Brain divisions
- Spinal cord
- Meninges, ventricles, CSF

### Topic 7: Somatic Sensory System (4 hours)
- Receptors
- Dorsal column-medial lemniscus pathway
- Somatosensory cortex
- Pain pathways

### Topic 8: Chemical Senses (3 hours)
- Taste (gustation)
- Smell (olfaction)
- Pheromones

### Topic 9: Auditory System (3 hours)
- Ear anatomy
- Cochlea and hair cells
- Auditory pathway
- Frequency and intensity coding

### Topic 10: Vision (5 hours)
- Eye anatomy
- Retina and photoreceptors
- Visual pathway
- Visual cortex (V1, V2, V4, IT)
- Color, motion, form processing

### Topic 11: Motor Control (5 hours)
- Spinal control (reflexes)
- Brainstem control
- Cortical control (motor cortex)
- Basal ganglia
- Cerebellum

### Topic 12: Development of Nervous System (3 hours)
- Neurogenesis
- Migration
- Differentiation
- Synaptogenesis
- Pruning

### Topic 13: Memory Systems (3 hours)
- Declarative vs. non-declarative
- Hippocampus
- Amygdala
- Cerebellum
- Cortical consolidation

### Topic 14: Emotion and Attention (3 hours)
- Limbic system
- Amygdala and fear
- Prefrontal cortex and regulation
- Attention networks (Posner)

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Neuron | Nerve cell |
| Action potential | Brief electrical signal |
| Synapse | Junction between neurons |
| Neurotransmitter | Chemical messenger |
| Resting potential | Voltage across membrane at rest (~-70mV) |
| Threshold | Voltage needed for action potential |
| Refractory period | Time after action potential |
| Saltatory conduction | Jumping between nodes of Ranvier |
| CNS | Central nervous system |
| PNS | Peripheral nervous system |
| Glia | Non-neuronal support cells |
| Myelin | Insulating sheath around axons |
| LTP | Long-term potentiation |
| Hippocampus | Memory structure |
| Amygdala | Fear and emotion |

## Self-Check Questions
