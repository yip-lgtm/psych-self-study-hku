# PSYC2005 — Introduction to Counselling and Therapeutic Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2005
- **Title:** Introduction to counselling and therapeutic psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course provides a theoretical and experiential foundation for counselling and further training in the mental health profession. Major theoretical approaches in psychotherapy are surveyed and critiqued. Experiential learning is used to help elucidate some of the course material and facilitate reflection. Topics covered also include basic counselling skills and practice, ethics, diversity and cultural values, indigenous and community-based models, and outcome and process research. Students who have taken SOWK0117 / SOWK2117 / SOWK2137 are not allowed to enrol PSYC2005.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Has experiential component; mutually exclusive with certain social work courses

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major theoretical approaches in psychotherapy
2. Demonstrate basic counselling skills
3. Apply ethical principles to therapeutic practice
4. Discuss cultural diversity in counselling
5. Evaluate outcome and process research in psychotherapy
6. Reflect on personal experience of therapeutic techniques
7. Critically appraise indigenous and community-based models

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Counselling (3 hours)
- Definition and history
- Counselling vs. psychotherapy vs. clinical psychology
- Roles of the counsellor
- Hong Kong counselling context

### Topic 2: Theoretical Approaches I — Psychodynamic and Humanistic (5 hours)
- Psychoanalytic therapy (Freud)
- Person-centred therapy (Rogers)
- Existential therapy
- Gestalt therapy

### Topic 3: Theoretical Approaches II — Cognitive-Behavioral (5 hours)
- Cognitive therapy (Beck)
- Rational emotive behavior therapy (Ellis)
- Behavioral therapy
- CBT (combined)

### Topic 4: Theoretical Approaches III — Systemic and Others (4 hours)
- Family systems therapy (Minuchin, Bowen)
- Narrative therapy
- Solution-focused therapy
- Mindfulness-based approaches (MBCT)

### Topic 5: Basic Counselling Skills (6 hours)
- Active listening
- Empathic responding
- Open and closed questions
- Reflection of content and feeling
- Paraphrasing and summarising
- Experiential practice (role-play)

### Topic 6: Ethics in Counselling (4 hours)
- Confidentiality
- Informed consent
- Boundaries and dual relationships
- Duty to warn/protect
- Cultural competence

### Topic 7: Diversity and Cultural Values (4 hours)
- Multicultural counselling
- Indigenous models (Chinese, HK context)
- Gender, sexuality, ability
- Religion and spirituality
- Social class

### Topic 8: Outcome Research (3 hours)
- Effectiveness of different therapies
- Common factors theory
- Evidence-based practice
- Treatment manuals

### Topic 9: Process Research (3 hours)
- Therapeutic alliance
- Empathy and outcomes
- Client factors
- Therapist effects

### Topic 10: Self-Reflection and Professional Development (3 hours)
- Self-care
- Burnout prevention
- Supervision
- Career paths in counselling

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Active listening | Fully concentrating on what the client is saying |
| Therapeutic alliance | Bond between therapist and client |
| Transference | Client projects past relationships onto therapist |
| Countertransference | Therapist's emotional reactions to client |
| Empathy | Understanding client's perspective |
| Unconditional positive regard | Acceptance without judgment (Rogers) |
| CBT | Cognitive-behavioral therapy |
| Common factors | Elements shared across therapies (alliance, empathy) |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare psychoanalytic and CBT approaches. What are the core differences?
2. What is the therapeutic alliance? Why does research emphasize it?
3. Describe 3 basic counselling skills. How would you practice them?
4. Discuss ethical issues in cultural adaptation of therapy.
5. What is the "Dodo bird verdict" in psychotherapy research?
