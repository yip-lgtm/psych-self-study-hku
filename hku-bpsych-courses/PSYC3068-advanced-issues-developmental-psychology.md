# PSYC3068 — Advanced Issues in Developmental Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3068
- **Title:** Advanced issues in developmental psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced seminar course reviews findings from both recent and classical research on developmental psychology. Modules will consider in-depth, select special topics such as language development, attachment, cognitive development, and moral development. Modules will be discussed from a multidisciplinary standpoint, integrating cognitive, sociocultural, and evolutionary approaches. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and either PSYC2009 or PSYC2059
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Seminar; advanced developmental psychology

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate recent and classical developmental psychology research
2. Discuss language development
3. Analyze attachment theory
4. Discuss cognitive development
5. Discuss moral development
6. Integrate cognitive, sociocultural, and evolutionary approaches
7. Conduct advanced developmental psychology research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Theories of Development (4 hours)
- Piaget (cognitive stages)
- Vygotsky (sociocultural)
- Information processing
- Dynamic systems
- Comparison

### Topic 2: Attachment (5 hours)
- Bowlby and Ainsworth
- Strange Situation
- Attachment types (secure, anxious, avoidant, disorganized)
- Adult attachment
- Cross-cultural variation
- HK Chinese context

### Topic 3: Language Development I (4 hours)
- Phonological development
- Lexical development
- Bilingual development
- Critical period hypothesis

### Topic 4: Language Development II (4 hours)
- Syntactic development
- Pragmatics
- Theory of mind and language
- Reading development and dyslexia

### Topic 5: Cognitive Development I — Infancy (4 hours)
- Piaget's sensorimotor stage
- Object permanence
- Habituation studies
- Numerical cognition

### Topic 6: Cognitive Development II — Childhood (4 hours)
- Piaget's preoperational and concrete operational
- Executive function development
- Memory development
- Theory of mind

### Topic 7: Cognitive Development III — Adolescence and Adulthood (4 hours)
- Formal operations
- Post-formal thought
- Adolescent brain
- Emerging adulthood
- Cognitive aging

### Topic 8: Moral Development (4 hours)
- Kohlberg's stages
- Gilligan's care perspective
- Haidt's moral foundations
- Cross-cultural variation
- Prosocial development

### Topic 9: Self and Identity Development (3 hours)
- Erikson's stages
- Self-concept
- Ethnic identity
- Identity in adolescence

### Topic 10: Social Development (4 hours)
- Peer relationships
- Family relationships
- Bullying
- Cyberbullying
- HK Chinese context (filial piety, education)

### Topic 11: Atypical Development (4 hours)
- Autism Spectrum Disorder
- ADHD
- Intellectual disability
- Specific language impairment

### Topic 12: Research Project (3 hours)
- Independent or small group
- Original data collection or analysis

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Attachment | Emotional bond |
| Secure base | From which to explore |
| Strange Situation | Ainsworth's lab procedure |
| Theory of mind | Understanding others' minds |
| Mentalizing | Reasoning about mental states |
| Filial piety | Confucian respect for parents |
| Object permanence | Objects exist when unseen |
| Conservation | Quantity unchanged by appearance |
| Concrete operations | Logical thinking about concrete |
| Formal operations | Abstract and hypothetical thinking |
| Identity | Self-definition |
| Adolescence | Transition to adulthood |
| Atypical development | Development outside norm |
| Sensitive period | Optimal time for development |
