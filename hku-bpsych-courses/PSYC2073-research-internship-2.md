# PSYC2073 — Research Internship in Psychology II

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2073
- **Title:** Research internship in psychology II
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** Please refer to Research internship in psychology I for the course description. Internship I and II can be done with the same supervisor or two different supervisors.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Continuation of PSYC2072; can be same or different supervisor

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Deepen research skills from PSYC2072
2. Take on more independent research responsibilities
3. Contribute to multiple aspects of a research project
4. Develop a more specialized research skill set
5. Build a more substantial research portfolio
6. Strengthen faculty relationships for future collaboration

## Topic Outline

**Similar structure to PSYC2072 but with more depth and independence.**

### Topic 1: Deeper Lab Integration (5-10 hours)
- Building on PSYC2072 relationships
- New responsibilities
- Mentoring newer lab members (if applicable)

### Topic 2: Advanced Reading (15-25 hours)
- Deeper into specific topics
- Reading latest preprints
- Engaging with theoretical debates

### Topic 3: Independent Project (30-50 hours)
- Lead a small study or component
- Design, collect, analyze
- Write up (possibly for conference or publication)

### Topic 4: Advanced Methods (20-30 hours)
- Specialize in lab's methods (e.g., EEG, eye tracking, fMRI, large-scale survey)
- Learn advanced analysis (e.g., SEM, MLM, time series)
- Develop technical skills

### Topic 5: Conference-Style Presentation (10-15 hours)
- Conference poster or talk
- Writing abstract
- Receiving feedback

### Topic 6: Drafting a Manuscript (10-15 hours)
- Contribute to a paper
- Possibly first-author on a small project
- Peer review process

### Topic 7: Internship Report (15-20 hours)
- 12-20 page report
- More substantial than PSYC2072
- May include a manuscript draft
