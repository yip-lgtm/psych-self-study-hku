# PSYC3053 — Advanced Research in Industrial/Organizational Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3053
- **Title:** Advanced research in industrial/organizational psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This research-based course focuses on specific topics in industrial/organizational psychology that are pertinent to the latest economic development in Hong Kong and in the Greater China Region. Psychometric assessment of various job attitudes, aptitudes, and work-related personality will be one such topic. Students will develop their knowledge and hands-on skills in selected areas covered in the introductory course on Industrial and Organizational Psychology. Students will each do an independent empirical research project. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and PSYC2063
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Greater China / HK focus; psychometric emphasis

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Conduct advanced I-O research
2. Apply psychometrics to workplace settings
3. Address Greater China / HK specific I-O issues
4. Design and execute empirical research
5. Evaluate I-O interventions
6. Apply I-O psychology to engineering/work contexts

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: I-O Psychology in Greater China (4 hours)
- Cultural context (Confucian, collectivist)
- Economic transitions
- HR practices in Chinese firms
- HK specific issues (e.g., cross-border work, talent retention)

### Topic 2: Job Attitudes in Greater China (4 hours)
- Job satisfaction
- Organizational commitment
- Engagement
- Cultural moderators
- Cross-cultural measurement

### Topic 3: Psychometric Assessment in Selection (5 hours)
- Cognitive ability tests
- Personality inventories (Big Five for selection)
- Integrity tests
- Work samples
- Assessment centers
- Adverse impact in multicultural contexts

### Topic 4: Job Performance (3 hours)
- Task and contextual performance
- Counterproductive work behavior
- Adaptive performance
- Performance measurement

### Topic 5: Work-Related Personality (3 hours)
- Big Five in workplace
- HEXACO and integrity
- Dark triad in workplace
- Personality-job fit

### Topic 6: Workplace Well-being (3 hours)
- Burnout
- Work-life balance
- Mental health at work
- Interventions
- Cross-cultural perspectives

### Topic 7: Leadership in Chinese Context (3 hours)
- Paternalistic leadership
- Transformational in Confucian cultures
- Implicit leadership theories
- Emerging leadership models

### Topic 8: Research Methods in I-O (4 hours)
- Survey design
- Cross-cultural translation
- Multi-level data (employees in teams in organizations)
- Field studies
- Intervention research

### Topic 9: Independent Research Project (10 hours)
- Topic selection
- Literature review
- Method design
- Data collection
- Analysis
- Reporting

### Topic 10: Special Topics (3 hours)
- Selected by instructor
- Examples: gig economy, AI in HR, remote work, post-COVID I-O

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| I-O psychology | Industrial-organizational psychology |
| Job satisfaction | Positive attitude toward job |
| Organizational commitment | Bond to organization |
| Performance | Behaviors contributing to goals |
| Engagement | Energy, involvement, efficacy at work |
| Personality-job fit | Match between person and job |
| Paternalistic leadership | Authoritative + benevolent + moral |
| Multilevel modeling | Handles nested data |
| Cross-cultural | Across cultures |
| Adverse impact | Disproportionate negative effect |
