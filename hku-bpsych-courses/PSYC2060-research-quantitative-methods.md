# PSYC2060 — Research and Quantitative Methods in Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2060
- **Title:** Research and quantitative methods in psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course is designed to extend the skills and knowledge of students on research design and quantitative methods in psychology, so that they are better prepared to conduct their own independent empirical research. The course builds on what students already learned in PSYC1004 to cover more advanced topics.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001 and PSYC1004
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Builds on PSYC1004; prerequisite for thesis and advanced methods

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Design sophisticated psychological research
2. Apply advanced statistical tests
3. Conduct and interpret ANOVA
4. Conduct and interpret multiple regression
5. Use statistical software for analysis
6. Write research reports in APA format
7. Critically evaluate research design choices

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Review of PSYC1004 (3 hours)
- Hypothesis testing
- t-tests

- Standardized coefficients

### Topic 7: ANCOVA (3 hours)

- Assumptions
- Reporting

- Effect sizes

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| ANOVA | Analysis of variance |
| F-statistic | Ratio of between-group to within-group variance |
| Effect size | Standardized magnitude of effect |
| η² (eta squared) | Proportion of variance explained |
| R² | Variance explained in regression |
| Multicollinearity | High correlation among predictors |
| VIF | Variance Inflation Factor |
| ANCOVA | ANOVA with covariates |
| Mediation | Indirect effect through a third variable |
| Moderation | Effect of IV depends on another variable |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Conduct a one-way ANOVA interpretation. F(2, 87) = 4.32, p = .016, η² = 0.09.
2. Compare main effects and interactions in factorial ANOVA.
3. What is multicollinearity? How do you detect and address it?
4. Conduct a power analysis for a study with d = 0.5, α = .05, power = .80.
5. Write a results section for a multiple regression analysis.
