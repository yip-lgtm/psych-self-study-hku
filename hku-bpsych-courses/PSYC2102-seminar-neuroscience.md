# PSYC2102 — Seminar in Neuroscience

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2102
- **Title:** Seminar in neuroscience
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course surveys key topics in neuroscience research. Students will be reading, analysing, and evaluating classical and cutting-edge research studies. Throughout the course, students will be able to learn research methods in cellular and system neuroscience, and their applications in research of neurological and psychiatric syndromes. Students will be able to apply the basic concepts and technological knowledge on various experimental methods for neuroscience investigation, as well as be able to critically evaluate empirical research. In-class activities will include presentations and discussions, among other activities. (Priority will be given to students planning to major in neuroscience)
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC2101
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Reading- and presentation-heavy; advanced research methods

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Read and critique advanced neuroscience research
2. Discuss research methods in cellular and systems neuroscience
3. Apply methods to neurological/psychiatric conditions
4. Critically evaluate empirical research
5. Present research effectively
6. Engage in scholarly discussion
7. Identify promising research directions

## Topic Outline

**Topics vary. Representative structure:**

### Topic 1: Course Introduction (3 hours)
- Overview of current neuroscience
- Topic selection for presentations
- Reading groups formation

### Topic 2: Cellular Neuroscience Methods (5 hours)
- Electrophysiology (patch clamp, extracellular)
- Calcium imaging
- Optogenetics
- Chemogenetics
- Two-photon imaging
- Single-cell sequencing

### Topic 3: Systems Neuroscience Methods (5 hours)
- fMRI
- EEG/MEG
- ECoG
- TMS
- Lesion studies
- Animal models

### Topic 4: Molecular Neuroscience (4 hours)
- Gene expression (FISH, ISH, scRNA-seq)
- Transgenic animals
- CRISPR applications
- Connectomics

### Topic 5: Neurodegeneration (4 hours)
- Alzheimer's
- Parkinson's
- Huntington's
- ALS
- Prion diseases

### Topic 6: Psychiatric Disorders (5 hours)
- Schizophrenia
- Depression
- Anxiety
- OCD
- Autism
- Research methods challenges

### Topic 7: Cutting-Edge Research (4 hours)
- Recent publications
- High-profile replications
- Preprints and open science

### Topic 8: Student Presentations I (4 hours)
- Student-led discussions
- Peer feedback
- Critical engagement

### Topic 9: Student Presentations II (4 hours)
- Continued student presentations
- Synthesis across topics
- Discussion leadership

### Topic 10: Research Proposal Workshop (4 hours)
- Literature review
- Hypothesis development
- Method and analysis planning
- Grant-style writing

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Electrophysiology | Recording electrical activity |
| fMRI | Functional magnetic resonance imaging |
| EEG | Electroencephalography |
| Optogenetics | Controlling neurons with light |
| Chemogenetics | Controlling neurons with drugs (DREADDs) |
| Two-photon imaging | Deep tissue calcium imaging |
| Connectomics | Mapping neural connections |
| CRISPR | Gene editing |
| Patch clamp | Recording from single cells |
| Transgenic | Genetically modified organism |
| Single-cell sequencing | Profiling individual cells |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare the spatial and temporal resolution of fMRI and EEG.
2. How does optogenetics allow causal inference in neuroscience?
3. Discuss the challenges of modeling psychiatric disorders in animals.
4. What is connectomics? What are the major projects?
5. Reflect on a recent paper. What were its strengths and limitations?
