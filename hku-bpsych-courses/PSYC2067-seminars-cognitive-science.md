# PSYC2067 — Seminars in Cognitive Science

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2067
- **Title:** Seminars in cognitive science
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course is a tutorial-based reading course in specialist areas of cognitive science research and interest. It will include presentations and group discussion of research and issues of interest within the cognitive science approach to understanding intelligent systems.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC2066 or two disciplinary electives
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Tutorial-based; advanced reading; presentation required

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Read and critically evaluate advanced cognitive science literature
2. Synthesize research from multiple cognitive science disciplines
3. Present research findings effectively
4. Engage in scholarly discussion
5. Form and defend positions on cognitive science debates
6. Identify promising research directions

## Topic Outline

**Topics vary by instructor and current research. The following is a representative structure.**

### Topic 1: Course Introduction and Topic Selection (3 hours)
- Overview of current cognitive science debates
- Student interest surveys
- Topic selection for presentations

### Topic 2: Advanced Reading Skills (3 hours)
- Reading primary literature efficiently
- Critical evaluation frameworks
- Note-taking and synthesis

### Topic 3: Foundational Papers (5 hours)
- Reading and discussing classic cognitive science papers
- Identifying theoretical commitments
- Connecting to contemporary work

### Topic 4: Current Research in Cognitive Science (5 hours)
- Recent publications in top journals
- Hot topics and debates
- New methods and findings

### Topic 5: Student Presentations I (5 hours)
- Student-led discussions
- Peer feedback
- Critical engagement

### Topic 6: Student Presentations II (5 hours)
- Continued student presentations
- Synthesis across topics
- Discussion leadership

### Topic 7: Research Design Workshop (4 hours)
- Designing cognitive science research
- Cross-disciplinary methodology
- Practical considerations

### Topic 8: Writing Research Proposals (4 hours)
- Literature review
- Research questions and hypotheses
- Method and analysis plans

### Topic 9: Special Topics (5 hours)
- Selected by instructor
- May include: consciousness, AI, language, perception, decision making, etc.

### Topic 10: Final Synthesis and Discussion (3 hours)
- Integration of course themes
- Future directions in cognitive science
- Course wrap-up

## Self-Check Questions

1. What are the most important debates in cognitive science today?
2. How would you design a study to test [specific cognitive claim]?
3. What is the relationship between AI and cognitive science?
4. How does cognitive science inform our understanding of human uniqueness?
