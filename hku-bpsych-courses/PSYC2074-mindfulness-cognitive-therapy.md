# PSYC2074 — Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2074
- **Title:** Mindfulness-based cognitive therapy
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** The course has a strong experiential component. The standard 8-week program of mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT) is embedded in the course. Students have first-hand experience as the participants of the MBCT. They are required to do mindfulness practice 45-60 minutes daily and participate in a whole day silence retreat. In addition to this experiential learning, students also have the opportunities to study the theoretical background of meditative traditions and how mindfulness is applied in contemporary mental health services. This is a course that requires both practical involvement and theoretical understanding.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Strong experiential component; 45-60 min daily practice + silent retreat

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Practice mindfulness meditation daily
2. Describe the theoretical basis of MBCT
3. Apply MBCT principles to personal experience
4. Discuss the integration of mindfulness with CBT
5. Describe applications of mindfulness in mental health
6. Reflect on your own experience
7. Discuss cultural and historical roots of meditation

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Mindfulness (3 hours)
- Definition and history
- Buddhist origins
- Secular mindfulness
- Cultural adaptation

### Topic 2: Theoretical Foundations (4 hours)
- Mindfulness as attention regulation
- Mindfulness as emotion regulation
- Mindfulness as change in perspective
- Mechanisms of action (Hölzel et al., 2011)

### Topic 3: MBCT and Depression (4 hours)
- Relapse prevention in depression
- Teasdale's differential activation hypothesis
- Meta-analytic evidence
- Application to other conditions (anxiety, stress)

### Topic 4: The 8-Week MBCT Program (6 hours)
- Session-by-session structure
- Home practice
- Group process
- Teacher competencies

### Topic 5: Mindfulness Practices (10+ hours practice)
- Body scan
- Mindful breathing
- 3-minute breathing space
- Sitting meditation
- Mindful movement (yoga)
- Daily life practice

### Topic 6: Cognitive Components (3 hours)
- Thoughts as events, not facts
- De-centering
- Relapse signature recognition
- Linking with CBT techniques

### Topic 7: Whole-Day Silent Retreat (8 hours)
- Intensive practice
- Observation of mind
- Integration

### Topic 8: Self-Compassion Component (3 hours)
- Neff's self-compassion model
- Mindful self-compassion (MSC)
- Integration with MBCT

### Topic 9: Applications Beyond Depression (4 hours)
- Anxiety (MBSR, MBCT)
- Stress (MBSR)
- Chronic pain (MBI)
- Eating disorders
- Children and adolescents (MBCT-C, .b)

### Topic 10: Personal Reflection and Integration (3 hours)
- Your own experience
- Continued practice
- Ethical considerations
- Professional applications

## Required Resources

### Required Practice
- 45-60 minutes daily mindfulness practice (8 weeks)
- One whole-day silent retreat

### Texts
- **Segal, Williams, & Teasdale, "The Mindful Way through Depression"**
- **Kabat-Zinn, "Full Catastrophe Living"** (MBSR)
- **Neff, "Self-Compassion"**

### Key Papers
- Hölzel et al. (2011) — How does mindfulness work? (mechanisms)
- Khoury et al. (2013) — Meta-analysis of mindfulness-based therapy
- Teasdale et al. (2000) — MBCT for depression relapse

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Mindfulness | Non-judgmental present-moment awareness |
| MBCT | Mindfulness-based cognitive therapy |
| MBSR | Mindfulness-based stress reduction |
| De-centering | Seeing thoughts as mental events |
| Body scan | Lying meditation attending to body sensations |
| Sitting meditation | Formal seated practice |
| 3-minute breathing space | Brief practice for difficult moments |
| Relapse signature | Pattern of thoughts/moods signaling relapse |
| Self-compassion | Kindness toward oneself in suffering |
| Loving-kindness (metta) | Meditation cultivating warmth toward self/others |
| Beginner&#39;s mind | Approaching with fresh, open attitude |
| Non-striving | Not trying to achieve a particular state |
