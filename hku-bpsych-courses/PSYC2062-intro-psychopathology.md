# PSYC2062 — Introduction to Psychopathology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2062
- **Title:** Introduction to psychopathology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course will provide a broad exposure to both theory and practice in clinical psychology. It is a foundation course in mental health, counselling and other psychological services. A wide array of types of mental disorders will be examined. Important themes will be emphasized such as the continuum in behaviour from mental health to mental illness, the diathesis-stress and nature-nurture models and epidemiology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major categories of mental disorder
2. Apply the diathesis-stress model
3. Discuss nature-nurture in psychopathology
4. Interpret epidemiological data
5. Distinguish normal distress from disorder
6. Discuss ethical issues in mental health

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Historical Perspectives (3 hours)
- Demonology to medical model
- Asylums and deinstitutionalization
- DSM evolution
- Anti-psychiatry movement

### Topic 2: Classification (4 hours)
- DSM-5-TR
- ICD-11
- Categorical vs. dimensional
- RDoC
- Cultural formulation

### Topic 3: Etiological Frameworks (5 hours)
- Biopsychosocial model
- Diathesis-stress
- Gene-environment interaction
- Continuity from normal to abnormal

### Topic 4: Anxiety Disorders (4 hours)
- GAD, panic, phobias
- OCD and related
- Trauma and stressor-related
- Etiology and treatment overview

### Topic 5: Depressive and Bipolar (4 hours)
- Major depression
- Persistent depressive
- Bipolar I and II
- Etiology (monoamine, HPA, cognitive)

### Topic 6: Schizophrenia Spectrum (4 hours)
- Symptoms (positive, negative, disorganized)
- Course and prognosis
- Dopamine hypothesis
- Early intervention

### Topic 7: Personality Disorders (3 hours)
- Clusters A, B, C
- Borderline, Antisocial, Narcissistic
- DSM-5-TR vs. ICD-11 dimensional

### Topic 8: Substance Use and Eating Disorders (3 hours)
- Substance use disorder
- Alcohol use disorder
- Anorexia, bulimia, binge eating
- Etiology and treatment

### Topic 9: Epidemiology (3 hours)
- Prevalence and incidence
- Global burden
- Cross-cultural patterns
- Risk and protective factors

### Topic 10: Ethics and Practice (3 hours)
- Informed consent
- Confidentiality
- Duty to warn
- Cultural competence
- Stigma

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Psychopathology | Mental disorder pattern |
| Prevalence | Proportion with disorder |
| Diathesis-stress | Predisposition + stressor |
| Comorbidity | Co-occurring disorders |
| Positive symptoms | Additions to normal (hallucinations) |
| Negative symptoms | Subtractions (flat affect) |
| Continuum | View that normal and abnormal exist on a spectrum |
| Stigma | Negative attitudes toward mental illness |
| Recovery | Living a meaningful life despite disorder |
