# PSYC2019 — Psychology of Personality

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2019
- **Title:** Psychology of personality
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course will critically examine a number of theories of personality as exemplified in the lives of some of the significant figures in the field. The relationship of specific theories to practical applications, personality assessment and psychotherapeutic techniques may be included. The major aims of the course are to provide a survey of the breath and complexity of this field and to provide a perspective from which to examine assumptions about human nature and the evaluation of behaviour.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe major personality theories
2. Apply theories to historical figures and real people
3. Discuss the relationship between theory and assessment
4. Connect personality theory to therapeutic techniques
5. Critically evaluate personality research
6. Articulate your own assumptions about human nature

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: What is Personality? (3 hours)
- Definitions
- Levels of analysis
- Person vs. situation
- Idiographic vs. nomothetic

### Topic 2: Psychodynamic Approach (6 hours)
- Freud: structure, stages, defense mechanisms
- Case study: Anna O., Little Hans
- Jung: collective unconscious, archetypes
- Adler: inferiority, social interest
- Horney: neurosis, feminine psychology
- Object relations (Klein, Winnicott)

### Topic 3: Trait Theory (5 hours)
- Allport: cardinal, central, secondary
- Cattell: 16PF
- Eysenck: PEN model
- Big Five / Five-Factor Model
- HEXACO
- Factor analysis methodology

### Topic 4: Humanistic Approach (4 hours)
- Rogers: self-concept, conditions of worth
- Maslow: hierarchy of needs
- Self-actualization
- Critique: naive optimism?

### Topic 5: Social-Cognitive Approach (4 hours)
- Bandura: reciprocal determinism
- Mischel: cognitive-affective personality system
- Self-efficacy
- Cross-situational consistency

### Topic 6: Positive Psychology (3 hours)
- Character strengths (Peterson, Seligman)
- VIA Classification
- Grit (Duckworth)
- Mindset (Dweck)
- Flow (Csikszentmihalyi)

### Topic 7: Personality Assessment (4 hours)
- Self-report inventories (NEO, MMPI, 16PF)
- Projective tests (Rorschach, TAT)
- Behavioral assessment
- Implicit measures (IAT)
- Reliability and validity

### Topic 8: Personality Across the Lifespan (3 hours)
- Stability vs. change
- The "maturity principle"
- Cohort effects
- Personality in old age

### Topic 9: Personality in Context (3 hours)
- Personality and work
- Personality and relationships
- Personality and health
- Cultural variations

### Topic 10: Contemporary Issues (3 hours)
- Dark triad
- Personality disorders
- Digital phenotyping
- Personality and AI

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Personality | Enduring patterns of thought, feeling, behavior |
| Trait | Stable tendency |
| Big Five | O, C, E, A, N |
| Self-concept | Beliefs about self |
| Self-efficacy | Belief in capability |
| Defense mechanism | Unconscious ego protection |
| Psychosexual stages | Freud's developmental theory |
| Id/Ego/Superego | Freudian structure |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare Freud's and Rogers' theories of personality.
2. What is the Big Five? What evidence supports it?
3. Discuss the stability of personality across the lifespan.
4. Apply social-cognitive theory to a real person (e.g., yourself).
5. Compare self-report and projective measures of personality.
