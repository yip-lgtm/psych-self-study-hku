# PSYC2038 — Psychology of Language

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2038
- **Title:** Psychology of language
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** A study of the cognitive processes involved in language comprehension and production, including the acquisition of native as well as second languages, the biological and cognitive bases of language learning and use, the psychology of reading and reading disabilities, the comparison of psychological aspects of the Chinese language and other languages, and bilingualism.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 40% coursework and 60% examination
- **Notes:** Includes Chinese language perspective; has exam component

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe language processes (comprehension, production)
2. Explain first and second language acquisition
3. Discuss biological and cognitive bases of language
4. Describe reading processes and reading disabilities
5. Compare Chinese and other languages psychologically
6. Discuss bilingualism and its cognitive effects

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction (3 hours)
- Language and cognition
- Levels of linguistic analysis
- Methods in psycholinguistics

### Topic 2: Speech Perception (4 hours)
- Acoustic phonetics
- Categorical perception
- McGurk effect
- Word recognition (logogen model)

### Topic 3: Speech Production (4 hours)
- Levelt's model
- Tip-of-the-tongue phenomenon
- Slips of the tongue
- Lexical selection

### Topic 4: First Language Acquisition (5 hours)
- Stages (cooing, babbling, one-word, two-word, telegraphic)
- Nativist (Chomsky, LAD)
- Learning theories
- Social-pragmatic (Tomasello)
- Critical period hypothesis

### Topic 5: Second Language Acquisition (4 hours)
- Critical period for L2
- Age effects
- Input and interaction
- Bilingual education models

### Topic 6: Comprehension (4 hours)
- Parsing
- Garden-path sentences
- Discourse processing
- Pragmatics and inference

### Topic 7: Reading (4 hours)
- Word recognition (dual-route model)
- Reading development
- Dyslexia
- Chinese reading (logographic vs. alphabetic)

### Topic 8: Bilingualism (4 hours)
- Types of bilingualism
- Bilingual lexicon
- Code-switching
- Cognitive effects of bilingualism (executive function boost)
- Bilingual education in HK

### Topic 9: Chinese Language Specifics (3 hours)
- Logographic vs. alphabetic writing
- Chinese character processing
- Tonal processing
- Reading in Chinese

### Topic 10: Language and Thought (3 hours)
- Sapir-Whorf hypothesis (linguistic relativity)
- Color naming
- Counter-evidence
- Modern reformulations

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Phoneme | Smallest unit of sound |
| Morpheme | Smallest unit of meaning |
| Syntax | Sentence structure |
| Semantics | Meaning |
| Pragmatics | Context-based meaning |
| LAD | Language Acquisition Device (Chomsky) |
| Critical period | Time window for language acquisition |
| Bilingualism | Speaking two languages |
| Code-switching | Alternating between languages |
| Logographic | Writing system using symbols for whole words |
| Dyslexia | Reading disability |
| Tip-of-the-tongue | Inability to retrieve a known word |
