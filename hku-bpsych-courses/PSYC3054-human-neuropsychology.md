# PSYC3054 — Human Neuropsychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3054
- **Title:** Human neuropsychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course introduces basic principles of brain-behaviour relationships. Research methods for investigating brain-behavior relationships are reviewed. The neuro-anatomical and neuropsychological mechanisms underpinning various cognitive and affective processes as well as how these processes are dysregulated in some common brain disorders are discussed. Students will participate in an independent empirical research project. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology and neuroscience.
- **Prerequisites:** Either PSYC2101 or PSYC2022
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Independent empirical research project required

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe brain-behavior relationships
2. Discuss research methods in neuropsychology
3. Explain neuroanatomical and neuropsychological mechanisms
4. Apply knowledge to common brain disorders
5. Conduct independent empirical neuropsychology research
6. Critically evaluate neuropsychology research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Neuropsychology (3 hours)
- History
- Methods (lesion studies, neuroimaging, TMS)
- Mind-brain relationship

### Topic 2: Neuroanatomy for Neuropsychology (5 hours)
- Cortical and subcortical structures
- White matter tracts
- Vascular territories
- Functional networks

### Topic 3: Frontal Lobe Functions (4 hours)
- Prefrontal cortex subdivisions
- Executive functions
- Working memory
- Decision making
- Personality and behavior

### Topic 4: Temporal Lobe Functions (4 hours)
- Auditory processing
- Memory (hippocampus)
- Language (Wernicke's)
- Face/object recognition
- Klüver-Bucy syndrome

### Topic 5: Parietal Lobe Functions (3 hours)
- Somatosensory
- Spatial attention
- Number processing
- Gerstmann syndrome

### Topic 6: Occipital Lobe Functions (3 hours)
- Visual processing
- Ventral and dorsal streams
- Agnosias
- Cortical blindness

### Topic 7: Subcortical Structures (4 hours)
- Basal ganglia (movement, cognition)
- Thalamus (relay)
- Amygdala (emotion)
- Hippocampus (memory)
- Cerebellum (cognition)

### Topic 8: Aphasia and Language Disorders (3 hours)
- Broca's, Wernicke's, conduction
- Other aphasias
- Assessment and treatment

### Topic 9: Memory Disorders (3 hours)
- Amnesia (anterograde, retrograde)
- Alzheimer's disease
- Other dementias
- Assessment

### Topic 10: Common Brain Disorders (5 hours)
- Stroke
- Traumatic brain injury
- Parkinson's disease
- Multiple sclerosis
- Epilepsy
- Tumors

### Topic 11: Independent Research Project (8 hours)
- Topic selection
- Method
- Data collection/analysis
- Reporting

### Topic 12: Recent Advances (3 hours)
- Neuroplasticity
- Brain-computer interfaces
- Cognitive rehabilitation
- New treatments
