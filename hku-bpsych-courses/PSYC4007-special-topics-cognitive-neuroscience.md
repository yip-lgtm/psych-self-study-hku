# PSYC4007 — Special Topics in Psychology I (Cognitive Neuroscience)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC4007
- **Title:** Special topics in psychology I (cognitive neuroscience)
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced seminar course focuses on a specific topic at the cutting edge of psychology research. Topic varies by offering. Possible topics include advanced cognitive neuroscience, computational psychiatry, social neuroscience, neuroeconomics, consciousness science, and other emerging areas. May involve substantial reading, original research, and presentation. Restricted to final-year BPsych students.
- **Prerequisites:** Variable, typically Year 3 standing and 2000-level foundation
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** 4000-level honours seminar; topic varies

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Engage deeply with a specialized topic at the research frontier
2. Read and synthesize primary literature in the area
3. Conduct original research or analysis
4. Present research findings
5. Critically evaluate theoretical and methodological issues
6. Connect specialized topic to broader psychology

## Topic Outline (Example: Cognitive Neuroscience)

### Topic 1: Course Introduction (3 hours)
- Topic scope
- Reading list
- Student presentations
- Research project scope

### Topic 2: Foundations of Cognitive Neuroscience (4 hours)
- Cellular to systems
- Imaging methods
- Cognitive architecture

### Topic 3: Attention (4 hours)
- Attention networks (Posner)
- Executive attention
- Sustained attention
- Disorders of attention

### Topic 4: Memory (4 hours)
- Memory systems
- Hippocampal function
- Working memory
- Disorders of memory

### Topic 5: Decision Making (4 hours)
- Reward circuitry
- Reinforcement learning in the brain
- Risk and uncertainty
- Computational models

### Topic 6: Social Neuroscience (4 hours)
- Mentalizing network
- Mirror neurons
- Empathy
- Social cognition

### Topic 7: Consciousness (4 hours)
- Neural correlates of consciousness
- Theories of consciousness
- Disorders of consciousness
- Experimental approaches

### Topic 8: Computational Psychiatry (3 hours)
- Computational models of disorder
- RDoC framework
- Translational opportunities
- Limitations

### Topic 9: Neuroethics (3 hours)
- Brain stimulation
- Enhancement
- Privacy (neuroimaging)
- Free will

### Topic 10: Research Project (10 hours)
- Original research
- Conference-style presentation
- Final paper
