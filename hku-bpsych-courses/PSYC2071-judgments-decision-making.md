# PSYC2071 — Judgments and Decision Making

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2071
- **Title:** Judgments and decision making
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** To understand the psychological factors involved with human judgment and decision making. We will contrast human decision making with normative theories of rational choice, and survey psychological evidence of systematic decision biases and errors in judgments. We will discuss the heuristics and biases approach to judgment and decision errors, and critiques of this approach. Real world examples will be presented from the domains of medicine, economics, and consumer choice.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Direct fit with JDM subfield; matches trading interests

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe normative theories of rational choice
2. Explain heuristics and biases in judgment
3. Apply JDM theory to real-world domains (medicine, economics, consumer)
4. Critically evaluate the heuristics-and-biases approach
5. Discuss alternative frameworks (e.g., ecological rationality)
6. Design research on decision making

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to JDM (3 hours)
- Normative vs. descriptive theories
- The "rational" model
- Bounded rationality (Simon)
- History of JDM research

### Topic 2: Probability and Bayesian Reasoning (5 hours)
- Subjective probability
- Bayes' theorem
- Base rate neglect
- Conservatism
- Natural frequencies

### Topic 3: Heuristics and Biases I (5 hours)
- Availability heuristic
- Representativeness heuristic
- Anchoring and adjustment
- Affect heuristic
- Examples and demonstrations

### Topic 4: Prospect Theory (4 hours)
- Reference dependence
- Loss aversion
- Probability weighting function
- Framing effects
- Applications

### Topic 5: Dual-Process Theory (3 hours)
- System 1 and System 2
- Intuition vs. deliberation
- Cognitive reflection Test (CRT)
- Hot vs. cold cognition

### Topic 6: Judgment Under Uncertainty (3 hours)
- Confidence and overconfidence
- Calibration
- Prediction and forecasting
- Planning fallacy

### Topic 7: Decisions Over Time (3 hours)
- Intertemporal choice
- Discounting
- Present bias
- Hyperbolic discounting

### Topic 8: Decisions for Others (3 hours)
- Social preferences
- Altruism, fairness, reciprocity
- Ultimatum and dictator games
- Trust game

### Topic 9: Real-World Applications (5 hours)
- Medical decision making
- Financial decisions and behavioral finance
- Consumer choice
- Policy and nudge
- Engineering/technical decisions (relevant to your work)

### Topic 10: Critiques and Alternatives (3 hours)
- Gerd Gigerenzer's critique
- Fast and frugal heuristics
- Ecological rationality
- Adaptive behavior view
- Replication concerns in JDM

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Rational choice | Choosing option with highest expected utility |
| Bounded rationality | Limits on rational decision making |
| Heuristic | Mental shortcut |
| Bias | Systematic deviation from normativity |
| Availability heuristic | Judging by ease of recall |
| Representativeness | Judging by similarity to prototype |
| Anchoring | Influence of initial value |
| Loss aversion | Losses loom larger than gains |
| Framing | Effect of presentation on choice |
| Prospect theory | Descriptive theory under risk |
| Dual-process | System 1 (fast) and System 2 (slow) |
| Cognitive reflection | Tendency to override intuitive response |
| Ecological rationality | Heuristics as adaptive in environment |
