# PSYC4009 — Special Topics in Psychology III (Applied Social Psychology)

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC4009
- **Title:** Special topics in psychology III (applied social psychology)
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced seminar course focuses on a specific topic at the cutting edge of psychology research. Topic varies by offering. Possible topics include applied social psychology, health communication, social influence, organizational behavior, intergroup conflict resolution, and other emerging areas. May involve substantial reading, original research, and presentation. Restricted to final-year BPsych students.
- **Prerequisites:** Variable, typically Year 3 standing and 2000-level foundation
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** 4000-level honours seminar; topic varies

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Engage deeply with a specialized topic at the research frontier
2. Read and synthesize primary literature in the area
3. Conduct original research or analysis
4. Present research findings
5. Critically evaluate theoretical and methodological issues
6. Connect specialized topic to broader psychology
7. Apply psychological knowledge to real-world problems

- Reading list

## Self-Check Questions

1. How do you design a successful health communication campaign?
2. Discuss the ethics of nudging.
3. Evaluate the contact hypothesis for reducing prejudice.
4. What are the effects of social media on well-being?
5. Apply social psychology to a real-world problem (e.g., climate change).
