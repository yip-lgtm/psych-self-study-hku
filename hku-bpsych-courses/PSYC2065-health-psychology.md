# PSYC2065 — Health Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2065
- **Title:** Health psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course acquaints students with the realm of health psychology. Students will gain (a) an understanding of the ways psychosocial factors influence health concerns and healthcare utilization, and (b) familiarity with basic concepts that guide the work of health professionals. Topics covered in this course include health behaviors, coping with health-related stress, social support and health, psychoimmunology, management of chronic illnesses, and patient-practitioner interaction.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Very relevant to your MMA/fitness/self-regulation interests

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe the biopsychosocial model of health
2. Discuss health behavior theories
3. Explain coping and stress
4. Analyze social support and health
5. Discuss psychoimmunology
6. Address chronic illness management
7. Evaluate patient-practitioner communication

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Health Psychology (3 hours)
- Definition and history
- Biopsychosocial model
- Mind-body relationship
- Public health perspective

### Topic 2: Health Behaviors (5 hours)
- Smoking, diet, exercise, alcohol
- Health Belief Model
- Theory of Planned Behavior
- Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change)
- Self-determination theory in health
- Habit formation

### Topic 3: Stress and Coping (5 hours)
- Stress definitions
- Physiological stress response (HPA axis, cortisol)
- Lazarus and Folkman's transactional model
- Problem-focused vs. emotion-focused coping
- Coping flexibility
- Stress management interventions

### Topic 4: Social Support and Health (4 hours)
- Types of support (emotional, instrumental, informational)
- Buffering hypothesis
- Direct effects model
- Loneliness and health
- Online vs. offline support

### Topic 5: Psychoimmunology (4 hours)
- Stress and immune function
- PNI (psychoneuroimmunology)
- Inflammation and depression
- Behavioral immune system

### Topic 6: Chronic Illness Management (4 hours)
- Adherence
- Illness representations (Leventhal)
- Self-management
- Caregiver burden
- Quality of life

### Topic 7: Health Behavior Change Interventions (4 hours)
- Motivational interviewing
- CBT for health behaviors
- Technology-based interventions
- Nudge theory
- Cultural adaptation

### Topic 8: Patient-Practitioner Interaction (3 hours)
- Communication models
- Patient-centered care
- Health literacy
- Shared decision-making
- Cultural competence

### Topic 9: Pain (3 hours)
- Gate control theory
- Psychological factors
- Acute vs. chronic pain
- Management approaches

### Topic 10: End-of-Life and Palliative Care (3 hours)
- Death attitudes
- Hospice and palliative care
- Grief and bereavement
- Cultural perspectives
- Euthanasia debate

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Biopsychosocial | Biological, psychological, social factors |
| Health Belief Model | Perceived susceptibility, severity, benefits, barriers |
| Stages of Change | Precontemplation to maintenance |
| Coping | Managing stress |
| Problem-focused | Action to address stressor |
| Emotion-focused | Managing emotional response |
| Adherence | Following treatment plan |
| Self-regulation | Controlling one's behavior toward goals |
| Patient-centered care | Care responsive to patient preferences |
| PNI | Psychoneuroimmunology |
