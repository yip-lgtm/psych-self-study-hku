# PSYC3051 — Advanced Personality Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3051
- **Title:** Advanced personality psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced laboratory course is designed for students interested in acquiring advanced research and communication skills for conducting independent research and report findings to a broad audience. Students will develop skills in critically evaluating current theoretical controversies and methodological paradigms. Special attention is given to theoretical, methodological, and measurement issues such as critical thinking in personality psychology, personality research design, proposal writing, and research ethics. This course is conducted in a seminar format with the expectation that students will participate actively and on occasion help lead a discussion. Some combination of readings, written assignments, and oral presentation is required. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and either PSYC2019 or PSYC2020
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Lab course; seminar format; proposal writing
- **Mutually exclusive with:** PSYC3052

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate current personality controversies
2. Design and conduct independent personality research
3. Write a research proposal
4. Apply advanced research methods
5. Communicate research findings effectively
6. Discuss research ethics
7. Lead scholarly discussions

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Current Theoretical Controversies (5 hours)
- Big Five vs. HEXACO
- Trait vs. process
- Person vs. situation
- Evolutionary personality
- Personality neuroscience

### Topic 2: Research Design (5 hours)
- Cross-sectional vs. longitudinal
- Lab vs. online
- Implicit vs. explicit measures
- Behavioral measures
- Experience sampling

### Topic 3: Measurement Issues (5 hours)
- Factor structure debates
- Cross-cultural equivalence
- Response biases
- Implicit Association Test
- Behavioral residuals (SRP)

### Topic 4: Proposal Writing (5 hours)
- Research question
- Literature review
- Hypotheses
- Method
- Analysis plan
- APA style

### Topic 5: Advanced Methods (5 hours)
- Multilevel modeling
- Latent growth curve
- Network analysis
- Machine learning approaches
- Bayesian methods

### Topic 6: Communicating Research (4 hours)
- Writing papers
- Conference presentations
- Visualizing personality data
- Public communication

### Topic 7: Research Ethics (3 hours)
- IRB process
- Informed consent
- Data management
- Authorship
- Conflicts of interest

### Topic 8: Special Topics (6 hours)
- Selected by instructor
- Examples: dark triad, personality and health, personality across culture, personality and technology

### Topic 9: Student Research Projects (8 hours)
- Independent research
- Peer feedback
- Mentoring

### Topic 10: Student Presentations (4 hours)
- Final research project presentation
- Discussion leadership

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Big Five | Five-factor personality model |
| HEXACO | Six-factor model with Honesty-Humility |
| Trait | Stable behavioral tendency |
| Process | Dynamic personality expression |
| Experience sampling | Repeated measures in daily life |
| IAT | Implicit Association Test |
| SRP | Situation-Response Pattern |
| MLM | Multilevel modeling |
| Cross-cultural | Across cultures |
| Equivalence | Measurement invariance across groups |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Discuss a current theoretical controversy in personality psychology.
2. Design a study to test a specific personality hypothesis.
3. Compare explicit and implicit measures. When to use each?
4. How would you ensure cross-cultural validity in a personality study?
5. Write a 1-page research proposal for an independent project.
