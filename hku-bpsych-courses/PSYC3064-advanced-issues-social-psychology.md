# PSYC3064 — Advanced Issues in Social Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC3064
- **Title:** Advanced issues in social psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This advanced seminar course reviews findings from both recent and classical research on social psychology. Modules will consider in-depth, select special topics such as social cognition, attitudes and attitude change, prejudice and intergroup relations, and prosocial behavior. Modules will be discussed from a multidisciplinary standpoint, integrating social-cognitive, evolutionary, and sociocultural approaches. Priority will be given to UG students majoring in psychology.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1004 and PSYC2020
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Seminar; advanced social psychology

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Critically evaluate recent and classical social psychology research
2. Discuss social cognition
3. Apply theories of attitudes and attitude change
4. Analyze prejudice and intergroup relations
5. Discuss prosocial behavior
6. Integrate social-cognitive, evolutionary, and sociocultural approaches
7. Conduct advanced social psychology research

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Current Debates in Social Psychology (4 hours)
- Replication crisis update
- Implicit bias controversy
- Cultural variations
- WEIRD samples critique

### Topic 2: Social Cognition I — Foundations (4 hours)
- Heuristics and biases
- Schemas and stereotypes
- Automatic vs. controlled processing
- Dual-process models

### Topic 3: Social Cognition II — The Self (4 hours)
- Self-concept
- Self-esteem
- Self-regulation
- Self-presentation

### Topic 4: Attitudes and Attitude Change (5 hours)
- Structure of attitudes (ABC)
- Cognitive dissonance (Festinger)
- Elaboration Likelihood Model (Petty & Cacioppo)
- Self-perception theory (Bem)

### Topic 5: Persuasion (4 hours)
- Yale approach (Hovland)
- Cognitive approach (Petty & Cacioppo)
- Heuristic-Systematic Model (Chaiken)
- Subliminal persuasion

### Topic 6: Prejudice and Intergroup Relations (5 hours)
- Social identity theory (Tajfel)
- Self-categorization theory
- Stereotype content model
- Reducing prejudice (contact hypothesis)

### Topic 7: Prosocial Behavior (4 hours)
- Bystander effect
- Altruism
- Empathy-altruism hypothesis
- Social exchange theory

### Topic 8: Aggression (4 hours)
- Frustration-aggression hypothesis
- Social learning theory
- Deindividuation
- Recent research

### Topic 9: Attraction and Close Relationships (4 hours)
- Factors in attraction
- Attachment in adults
- Relationship maintenance
- Breakup

### Topic 10: Group Processes (4 hours)
- Social facilitation
- Group polarization
- Groupthink
- Conformity and obedience

### Topic 11: Cultural Social Psychology (4 hours)
- East-West differences
- Markus & Kitayama (independent vs. interdependent)
- Hofstede dimensions
- Hofstede and psychology
- Nisbett & Miyamoto (cognitive style)

### Topic 12: Research Project (3 hours)
- Independent or small group
- Original data collection or analysis
