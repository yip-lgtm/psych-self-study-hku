# PSYC2035 — Introduction to Educational Psychology

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2035
- **Title:** Introduction to educational psychology
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** This course focuses on how psychological theories are applied to learning, teaching, and facilitation of human growth. The topics include major developmental theories and their application to learning and instruction, learning theories from both behavioral and cognitive traditions, effective teaching methods and practices, learners' individual and group differences, achievement motivation, and assessment. Students will be involved in learning activities that require self-reflection and integration of daily life experience.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Experiential learning; reflective component

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Apply developmental theories to learning and instruction
2. Distinguish behavioral and cognitive learning theories
3. Describe effective teaching methods
4. Discuss individual and group differences in learning
5. Explain achievement motivation
6. Apply educational assessment principles
7. Reflect on personal learning experiences

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction (3 hours)
- What is educational psychology?
- Major theoretical traditions
- Research methods in education

### Topic 2: Developmental Theories and Learning (5 hours)
- Piaget and constructivism
- Vygotsky and sociocultural theory
- Bronfenbrenner's ecological systems
- Application: developmentally appropriate practice

### Topic 3: Behavioral Learning Theory (4 hours)
- Classical conditioning in education
- Operant conditioning (reinforcement, punishment)
- Application: classroom management, programmed instruction

### Topic 4: Cognitive Learning Theory (5 hours)
- Information processing
- Schema theory
- Cognitive load theory (Sweller)
- Multimedia learning (Mayer)
- Metacognition

### Topic 5: Social Learning Theory (3 hours)
- Bandura's observational learning
- Self-efficacy in academic settings
- Modeling in the classroom

### Topic 6: Individual Differences (4 hours)
- Intelligence theories
- Learning styles (controversial)
- Giftedness
- Learning disabilities (dyslexia, ADHD)

### Topic 7: Motivation (5 hours)
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation
- Self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan)
- Achievement goal theory
- Mindset (Dweck)
- Attribution theory (Weiner)
- Application: motivating students

### Topic 8: Effective Teaching Methods (4 hours)
- Direct instruction
- Inquiry-based learning
- Cooperative learning
- Mastery learning
- Differentiated instruction

### Topic 9: Classroom Management and Assessment (4 hours)
- Behavioral approaches
- Inclusive education
- Formative vs. summative assessment
- Test design

### Topic 10: Self-Reflection and Lifelong Learning (3 hours)
- Learning how to learn
- Self-regulated learning
- Reflective practice
- Applying to one's own education

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Constructivism | Knowledge is constructed by learner |
| Schema | Mental framework for organizing knowledge |
| Metacognition | Awareness and control of own learning |
| Self-efficacy | Belief in capability |
| Growth mindset | Belief that ability can develop |
| Fixed mindset | Belief that ability is static |
| Scaffolding | Temporary support |
| Zone of proximal development | Range of doable with help |
| Mastery orientation | Focus on learning vs. performance |
| Self-regulated learning | Active, constructive process |

## Self-Check Questions

1. Compare Piaget and Vygotsky. How do they inform teaching?
2. Explain operant conditioning. Apply it to a classroom scenario.
3. What is cognitive load theory? How should it inform instructional design?
4. Discuss the difference between growth and fixed mindset. Educational implications?
5. How would you design a lesson that addresses individual differences?
