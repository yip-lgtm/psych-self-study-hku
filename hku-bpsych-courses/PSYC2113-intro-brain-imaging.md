# PSYC2113 — Introduction to Brain Imaging

## Course Info (from HKU Catalog)

- **Code:** PSYC2113
- **Title:** Introduction to brain imaging
- **Credits:** 6
- **HKU Catalog Description:** Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) is widely used to study brain functions. This course is designed to provide a general introduction to the physical and physiological bases and principles of fMRI, MRI related safety issues, and design and analysis of fMRI experiments.
- **Prerequisites:** PSYC1001 and PSYC1004
- **Assessment:** 100% coursework
- **Notes:** Methods-focused; includes safety, design, analysis

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Describe physical and physiological bases of fMRI
2. Discuss MRI safety
3. Design fMRI experiments
4. Analyze fMRI data
5. Interpret fMRI results critically
6. Compare fMRI to other neuroimaging methods

## Topic Outline

### Topic 1: Introduction to Neuroimaging (3 hours)
- Brief history
- Comparison of methods (EEG, MEG, fMRI, PET, fNIRS, TMS)
- Choosing the right method

### Topic 2: Physics of MRI (5 hours)
- Nuclear magnetic resonance
- Magnetic field
- RF pulses
- Relaxation (T1, T2)
- Image formation
- Spatial encoding

### Topic 3: BOLD Signal (4 hours)
- Hemodynamic response
- Neurovascular coupling
- BOLD signal properties
- Limitations

### Topic 4: MRI Safety (3 hours)
- Magnetic field hazards
- Contraindications
- Screening procedures
- HK institutional safety protocols

### Topic 5: Experimental Design I (5 hours)
- Block designs
- Event-related designs
- Mixed designs
- Power analysis for fMRI

### Topic 6: Experimental Design II (4 hours)
- Control conditions
- Counterbalancing
- Stimulus presentation
- Behavioral data collection in scanner

### Topic 7: Preprocessing (5 hours)
- Motion correction
- Slice timing
- Coregistration
- Normalization
- Smoothing

### Topic 8: First-Level Analysis (5 hours)
- GLM
- Statistical parametric mapping
- Statistical thresholds (FWE, FDR)
- Multiple comparisons

### Topic 9: Group-Level Analysis (4 hours)
- Random effects
- Mixed effects
- Group inference
- Conjunction analyses

### Topic 10: Connectivity Analysis (3 hours)
- Functional connectivity
- Effective connectivity
- Resting-state fMRI
- Network analysis

### Topic 11: Critical Evaluation (3 hours)
- Reverse inference problem
- Multiple comparisons controversies
- Reporting standards
- Replication in neuroimaging

## Key Concepts Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| MRI | Magnetic resonance imaging |
| fMRI | Functional MRI |
| BOLD | Blood-oxygen-level-dependent |
| Hemodynamic response | Blood flow change with neural activity |
| Neurovascular coupling | Neural activity → blood flow |
| T1, T2 | Relaxation times |
| GLM | General linear model |
| SPM | Statistical parametric mapping |
| FWE | Family-wise error |
| FDR | False discovery rate |
| Preprocessing | Initial data cleaning |
| Coregistration | Aligning images |
| Normalization | Mapping to standard brain |
| Smoothing | Spatial filtering |
