# 6-Month Roadmap (Bachelor-equivalent → PhD prep)

> **Updated:** June 2026 — refined based on user input: 8 MSocSc(Psy) compulsory courses + Python tooling + site/slope/Minor Works/tender work experience context.

## Phase 0: Reality Check (read once, don't re-read)

**6 months CAN deliver:**
- Full coverage of **8 MSocSc(Psy) compulsory courses** (PSYC7301–7319) — these ARE the Bachelor-equivalent core for the conversion programme
- Research methods + stats competency (your edge — engineering + Python + Data Science cert)
- 1 small empirical project (survey → analysis → report)
- Application-ready knowledge + research statement
- **Bonus context:** site formation, slope stability, Minor Works, tender experience — surprisingly relevant to applied psych (judgment under uncertainty, project risk, professional decision-making)

**6 months CANNOT deliver:**
- Official transcript
- Lab experience (psychophysics, EEG, fMRI)
- Supervised practicum
- HKPS membership
- Clinical practice rights

**Honest path:** self-study → MSocSc(Psy) conversion (1 year) → MPhil/PhD or DPsych.

**Fallback path:** self-study → BPsych (4 years) → PhD. See `hku-msocs-psy/bpsych-as-alternative.md`.

## Monthly Milestones (high-level)

### Month 1 — Foundations + Research Methods
- [ ] OpenStax Psychology 2e: finish chapters 1–5 (Intro) + 2 (Research Methods)
- [ ] JASP installed; complete "Learning Statistics with JASP" chapters 1–6
- [ ] 1 mini analysis on public dataset (Kaggle/OSF mental health) — try JASP AND Python
- [ ] **Milestone:** Google Form survey designed (5–10 items, validated construct)

### Month 2 — Biological & Cognitive
- [ ] Noba Biological Psych module + Pinel selected chapters
- [ ] Noba Cognitive Psych module + Goldstein ch 1–6
- [ ] Draw concept map: "Brain → Behavior" (use Excalidraw, draw.io, or paper)
- [ ] **Milestone:** Read 3–5 classic papers with 300-word summary each (Müller-Lyer, Stroop, Kahneman & Tversky 1979, etc.)

### Month 3 — Developmental, Social, Personality
- [ ] Noba Developmental + Social + Personality modules
- [ ] Pick subtopic for 1500-word lit review (e.g., "Big Five and decision making in engineering", "Personality and self-regulated learning", "Mindset and athletic performance")
- [ ] **Milestone:** 1500-word lit review submitted (APA 7th, to self or peer)

### Month 4 — Abnormal + Subfield Elective
- [ ] Noba Psychopathology module
- [ ] Deep dive into chosen subfield (2–3 papers/week)
- [ ] Sample electives: PSYC7313 Health, PSYC7315 Psychometrics, PSYC7318 Educational, PSYC7310 Neuropsychology
- [ ] **Milestone:** 1 psychopathology case conceptualisation (diathesis-stress model format)

### Month 5 — Advanced + Academic Skills
- [ ] Zotero setup with APA 7th style
- [ ] 3-pass reading method internalized (5 papers minimum)
- [ ] CITI Program: complete "Responsible Conduct of Research" (Social & Behavioral) — FREE
- [ ] **Milestone:** Capstone proposal finalized (1–2 pages)

### Month 6 — Capstone + Application
- [ ] Run capstone study (survey live ≥ 2 weeks; N ≥ 50, target 100+)
- [ ] Analyze with JASP + R + Python (at least 2 of 3)
- [ ] Write 4000–6000 word report (intro, method, results, discussion, limitations, future direction — APA style)
- [ ] Personal Statement (1000 words) + CV + research proposal (1 page)
- [ ] **Milestone:** Submit HKU MSocSc(Psy) application

## Recommended Weekly Time Split

| Block | Hours | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon–Fri evenings** | 2h × 5 = 10h | Reading + Anki + light practice |
| **Sat morning** | 3h | Stats / data analysis deep work (JASP/R/Python) |
| **Sun morning** | 3h | Paper reading + writing |
| **Sun evening** | 1h | Weekly review + plan next week |
| **Buffer** | ~3h | Catch-up, or push into capstone |

Total: ~20h/week. Adjust based on work intensity (your slope/Minor Works/tender site work is unpredictable — leave buffer).

## Work-Experience Leverage (Your Hidden Asset)

Your engineering background isn't just "quantitative" — it gives you **specific real-world cases** for psychology concepts:

| Your Experience | Psychology Connection |
|---|---|
| Slope stability inspections | **Judgment under uncertainty** (Kahneman) — risk assessment with incomplete data |
| Minor Works project management | **Self-regulation + goal pursuit** — long-horizon projects without daily accountability |
| Tender preparation | **Decision-making under time pressure** — heuristics, biases, framing effects |
| Cross-discipline self-learning | **Metacognition + transfer of learning** — how to learn new domains efficiently |
| Site safety culture | **Social influence + group dynamics** — Asch, Milgram, Zimbardo in real workplaces |
| Multi-stakeholder communication | **Persuasion + attitude change** — Cialdini's principles in action |

**Use these in your Personal Statement.** "I'm not leaving engineering to do psychology — I'm bringing engineering's real-world decision contexts INTO psychology research."

## What Success Looks Like

- **By Month 3:** you can read a psychology paper and understand the method, stats, and conclusion
- **By Month 6:** you can design a study, run it, analyze it, write it up
- **By application time:** you have a coherent narrative: engineer → self-taught → research-track psychologist
- **Bonus signal:** capstone topic + research proposal aligned with HKU faculty interests (Human Neuroscience & AI, Clinical & Health, Developmental & Educational, Judgment & Decision Making)
